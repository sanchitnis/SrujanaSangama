## 1. Constitutional and University Authority

**REVA University Act, 2012 (Karnataka)**
- REVA is a deemed-to-be-university under UGC Act 1956 s. 3
- The Board of Management is the supreme authority; Vice Chancellor exercises executive authority
- Financial commitments beyond delegated powers require Board approval
- MOUs with foreign universities must be reported to UGC and state government

**Karnataka Private Universities Act, 2013** (as applicable)
- Governs private universities in Karnataka
- Autonomy in academic matters but subject to UGC norms

## 2. UGC and AICTE Regulations

**UGC (Institutions Deemed to be Universities) Regulations, 2019**
- Deemed universities may enter MOUs with foreign universities for academic collaboration
- Joint/dual degree programs with foreign universities require UGC prior approval (Regulation 22)
- Twinning programs require specific UGC sanction

**UGC Guidelines on Transnational Education (2023 draft)**
- Foreign higher education institutions operating in India require UGC approval
- MOUs enabling foreign degrees to be awarded in India without approval are void

**UGC Credit Framework for Academic Bank of Credits, 2021**
- Any credit transfer arrangement must align with ABC norms
- Minimum credit equivalency norms apply for student exchange credits

**AICTE Approval**
- Required for any new program (including joint/collaborative) in technical disciplines
- Industry partner co-designed programs must comply with AICTE Model Curriculum guidelines

## 3. Intellectual Property Laws

**Patents Act, 1970 (as amended 2005)**
- s. 6: Inventions by employees in the course of employment vest in employer unless agreed otherwise
- s. 20: Government/public institution inventions — specific provisions apply
- Collaborative research: Ownership must be agreed in writing; absent agreement, joint inventorship rules apply
- REVA should ensure its IP Policy aligns with any MOU IP clause

**Copyright Act, 1957**
- s. 17: Works made in the course of employment vest in employer
- s. 19: Assignment must be in writing and signed; oral assignments are void
- s. 57: Moral rights of authors are inalienable — cannot be contracted away
- Course materials, lecture notes, research papers: Copyright vests in REVA (employer) or faculty (if created independently)

**Trade Secrets**
- India has no standalone trade secrets statute; protection through contract (confidentiality clauses) and tort law
- Reasonable duration and scope limitations are required for enforceability

## 4. Data Protection — Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA)

**Key provisions for REVA MOUs:**

**s. 4 — Grounds for processing**: Personal data of students/faculty may be processed only for a lawful purpose with consent or legitimate use
**s. 7 — Consent**: Must be free, specific, informed, unconditional, and unambiguous
**s. 8 — Data Fiduciary obligations**: REVA as Data Fiduciary must ensure accuracy, security, and deletion when purpose is fulfilled
**s. 9 — Children's data**: Students under 18 are children; verifiable parental consent required; no behavioural tracking

**For MOUs involving personal data:**
- Identify if REVA is Data Fiduciary (determines purpose) or Data Processor (processes on instruction)
- If sharing student data with industry partner: Partner becomes Data Processor; Data Processing Agreement required
- Cross-border transfer (foreign university): Permitted to countries notified by Central Government; check current whitelist
- Breach notification: REVA must notify the Data Protection Board and affected individuals as per notified timelines (expected: 72 hours for Board; reasonable time for individuals)

**Data localisation**: No blanket localisation requirement under DPDPA 2023, but sensitive data categories may be added by notification

**Practical implication for MOUs**: Any MOU where student placement data, academic records, health data, or biometric data is shared must include a DPDPA-compliant data processing clause or separate DPA annex.

## 5. Foreign Collaboration — FCRA and FEMA

**Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010 (FCRA)**
- Applies when a "foreign source" provides money, articles, or other contributions
- Foreign university = foreign source under FCRA
- If a foreign university provides: grants, scholarships, equipment, or funds to REVA → FCRA registration or prior permission required
- Pure academic exchange (no money) → generally outside FCRA scope
- Student fees paid by foreign students → not foreign contribution (commercial transaction)

**FCRA exemption**: Exchange of purely academic information, sending of gifts of educational materials within prescribed limits — check current FCRA rules for exemption thresholds

**Action**: Any MOU with a foreign university involving financial flows should be reviewed by REVA's legal counsel for FCRA applicability before signing.

**Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 (FEMA)**
- Governs cross-border payments
- Foreign currency transactions for services (consultancy, research) must be routed through authorised dealer banks
- Overseas Direct Investment (ODI) rules apply if REVA invests in foreign entities
- Inward remittances for research/consultancy: Permitted; report to RBI as required

## 6. Tax — GST and Income Tax

**Goods and Services Tax (GST)**
- **Exempt services**: Services by educational institutions to students, faculty, and staff
- **Taxable services**: Consultancy, sponsored research, testing services rendered by REVA to industry partners — 18% GST applies
- **Export of services**: Services to foreign universities may qualify as "export" if payment received in foreign exchange — 0% GST
- **MOU implication**: If MOU involves REVA providing taxable services, GST clause must specify who bears GST; absence creates dispute risk

**Income Tax**
- REVA as a university may claim exemption under s. 10(23C) of Income Tax Act 1961
- Accumulation of income beyond permissible limits or use for non-educational purposes jeopardises exemption
- Surplus from industry collaborations: Must be applied to educational purposes

## 7. Arbitration and Dispute Resolution

**Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 (as amended 2021)**
- Domestic arbitration: Seat in India; enforceable as a decree
- International commercial arbitration: At least one party is foreign; different rules apply
- Institutional arbitration preferred: DIAC (Delhi), MCIA (Mumbai), or BCCI (Bengaluru)
- Ad hoc arbitration is acceptable for lower-value disputes

**Mediation Act, 2023**
- Establishes statutory framework for mediation in India
- Pre-litigation mediation is now available and encouraged
- Mediated settlement agreements are final and binding (subject to fraud/void provisions)
- REVA should include mediation as a step before arbitration in all domestic MOUs

**Recommended REVA dispute resolution clause (domestic)**:
> *"In the event of any dispute arising out of or in connection with this MOU, the parties shall first attempt resolution through good-faith negotiation within 30 days of notice. If unresolved, the parties shall submit the dispute to mediation under the Mediation Act, 2023, with a mediator appointed by mutual consent, for a period not exceeding 30 days. If mediation fails, the dispute shall be referred to arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, with a sole arbitrator appointed by mutual agreement, with the seat of arbitration at Bengaluru, Karnataka. The proceedings shall be conducted in English. The award shall be final and binding."*

## 8. Anti-Corruption and Ethics

**Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 (as amended 2018)**
- Applies to public servants; REVA officials may be classified as public servants in certain contexts
- Commercial advantage given to REVA in exchange for preferential treatment = corruption risk

**For Foreign Partners**
- UK Bribery Act 2010: Applies to UK companies globally; REVA as counterparty should ensure no facilitation payments are expected
- US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA): Applies to US companies; similar risk
- REVA MOU should include a mutual anti-bribery/anti-corruption representation

**Recommended clause**:
> *"Each party represents and warrants that it has not made, and shall not make, any payment or provide any benefit to any government official, employee, or any other person in connection with this MOU that would constitute a violation of any applicable anti-corruption or anti-bribery law."*
