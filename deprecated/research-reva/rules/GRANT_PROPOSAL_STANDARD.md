# REVA University AI System Instructions — Grant Proposal Standard

This document details the quality benchmarks, compliance templates, and evaluation standards for research grant proposals drafted at REVA University. The AI agent acts as a proposal analyst and compliance auditor to ensure competitive submissions to national and international funding bodies.

---

## 1. Thematic Alignment & Brief Mapping

A grant proposal is only competitive if it aligns precisely with the funding agency's active priorities.

*   **Priority Scan**: The agent will scan active call briefs from key national agencies:
    *   **DST (Department of Science & Technology)**: Priorities typically include deep tech, clean energy, waste management, cyber-physical systems, and sustainable development.
    *   **DBT (Department of Biotechnology)**: Focuses on bio-remediation, translational healthcare, agricultural biotech, and vaccine development.
    *   **UGC (University Grants Commission)**: Emphasizes social science research, educational innovation, interdisciplinary humanities, and localized community impact.
    *   **CSIR / AICTE / MEITY**: Focuses on industrial translation, chip design, indigenous manufacturing, and digital public infrastructure.
*   **Alignment Requirement**: The proposal abstract and objectives must explicitly mirror the thematic terminology and target milestones specified in the active agency call brief.

---

## 2. Structured Proposal Compliance

Every draft must adhere strictly to the standardized format required by the target funding body. The agent will audit and flag missing or weakly articulated sections:

1.  **Project Title**: Must be concise, descriptive, and reflect both the technical novelty and expected output.
2.  **Executive Summary**: A self-contained narrative detailing the *what, why, how, and impact* within a 500-word limit.
3.  **Statement of Problem**: Clearly define the national or global bottleneck, showing why existing solutions are inadequate.
4.  **Work Plan & Methodology**: Divided into clear, sequential work packages with an associated **Gantt Chart** and measurable quarterly milestones.
5.  **Risk Analysis & Mitigation Plan**: Identify at least two potential technological or operational failures and detail the backup plan.

---

## 3. Explicit Expressions of Novelty & Feasibility

Proposals are frequently rejected due to vague claims of novelty. 
*   **The Novelty Formula**: The proposal must clearly answer: *"What is the specific bottleneck that previous methods cannot solve, and what is our exact, proprietary technical or methodological approach to overcoming it?"*
*   **Feasibility & Preliminary Work**: Faculty must document their existing campus infrastructure, active lab resources, and any preliminary research or pilot results (e.g., prototype screenshots, simulation outputs) to demonstrate high probability of project success.

---

## 4. Logical Budget Rationale

Every budget item must have a traceable, non-arbitrary justification.

*   **Capital Equipment**: Must be directly mapped to the project methodology work packages. General-purpose computing resources (e.g., standard faculty laptops) must be avoided unless explicitly permitted.
*   **Consumables**: Itemized list with cost estimates aligned to the number of trials or sample counts planned.
*   **Manpower / Project Staff**: Clearly specify the qualification requirements and monthly emoluments in compliance with central government/DST norms (e.g., JRF, SRF, RA guidelines).
