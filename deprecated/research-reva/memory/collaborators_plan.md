# Collaborator & Co-PI Strategy: Project Srujana-Drishti

To make the DST-SERB Core Research Grant (CRG) proposal highly competitive, the team must address a common reviewer question: **"How do you validate that computer vision metrics (gaze, posture) actually map to real cognitive engagement and learning outcomes?"**

Answering this requires an interdisciplinary team. While you (HBK) serve as the **Principal Investigator (PI)** leading the **Computer Vision & Systems Architecture**, you should onboard Co-PIs/Collaborators covering two essential domains.

---

## 1. Required Co-PI Profile: Pedagogy & Educational Technology
This collaborator will design the validation protocols, map the AI-generated indices to established learning taxonomies, and measure the pedagogical impact of the dashboard on faculty teaching methods.

### Key Responsibilities in the Project
* Designing classroom active-learning experiments to calibrate the attention index.
* Validating whether high Classroom Attention Index (CAI) scores correlate with student performance (grades, concept retention).
* Helping structure the user interface (UI) of the faculty dashboard to ensure it is pedagogically useful.

### Where to Find Them at REVA University
* **School of Education:** Faculty specializing in Educational Technology, instructional design, or learning taxonomies.
* **Senior Faculty in Engineering Education:** Senior professors who have published research on active learning strategies or engineering pedagogy.

---

## 2. Required Co-PI / Expert Profile: Cognitive Psychology or Neuroscience
This collaborator will ensure that the computer vision metrics (e.g., micro-expressions, gaze duration) are grounded in established scientific theories of human attention, focus, and cognitive load.

### Key Responsibilities in the Project
* Grounding the multi-modal fusion index (gaze + posture + facial micro-expressions) in cognitive psychology models (such as Sweller’s Cognitive Load Theory).
* Designing control group experiments using baseline gold-standard psychological metrics (e.g., self-report questionnaires, standardized attention tests).
* Addressing ethics and psychological safety guidelines for student participants.

### Where to Find Them
* **Internal (REVA University):** 
  * **School of Arts, Humanities, and Social Sciences (Department of Psychology):** Faculty members researching cognitive psychology, behavioral sciences, or human development.
  * **School of Allied Health Sciences:** Faculty with expertise in clinical psychology or neuro-rehabilitation.
* **External (Bengaluru Ecosystem):**
  * **NIMHANS (National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences):** Department of Cognitive Psychology or Neurophysiology (excellent for joint DST-SERB proposals to show high-tier collaboration).
  * **IISc (Indian Institute of Science):** Center for Neuroscience or Centre for Product Design and Manufacturing (CPDM) working on human-computer interaction (HCI).
