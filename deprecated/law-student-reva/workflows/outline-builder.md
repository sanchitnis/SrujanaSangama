# /outline-builder [subject]

**Usage:** `/outline-builder Constitutional Law` or `/outline-builder Indian Contract Act`

Build a subject outline **scaffold** — topic tree, sub-topic slots, case placeholders — and then ask Socratic questions as the student fills rules from their own notes and casebook.

**This workflow will NOT auto-generate a populated outline from a syllabus alone.** If the student pastes a syllabus and asks for a complete outline, decline:
> "I can give you the topic tree and the structure — the rules, cases, and analysis have to come from you. That's the point. Let's start with the topic tree and you fill it in as we go."

---

## Profile Check

- Subject from student profile: confidence level (strong / okay / shaky / avoid)
- Learning style: drill-me vs. explain-to-me
- If "shaky" or "avoid": note it and tell the student this outline session is for building foundations, not polishing

---

## Step 1 — Ask What the Student Has

> Before I build the topic tree, tell me:
> 1. Do you have your syllabus or lecture notes for this subject? (Paste or path)
> 2. Do you already have a partial outline you want to extend, or are we starting fresh?
> 3. What is your exam date for this subject?

If the student has notes/syllabus: use them to **anchor the topic tree** to the actual units the professor will examine. If not: use the standard law school syllabus for that subject.

---

## Step 2 — Build the Topic Tree

Generate the topic tree (skeleton only — no rules, no cases, no analysis):

**Example — Indian Contract Act:**
```
INDIAN CONTRACT ACT, 1872

1. FORMATION OF CONTRACT
   1.1 Offer (Proposal)
       - Definition [S.2(a)] — student fills
       - Rules of valid offer — student fills
       - Specific offer vs. general offer — student fills
       - Key cases: [blank] — student fills
   1.2 Acceptance
       - Definition [S.2(b)] — student fills
       - Rules of valid acceptance — student fills
       - Communication of acceptance — student fills
       - Key cases: [blank] — student fills
   1.3 Consideration
       ...

2. ESSENTIAL ELEMENTS [S.10]
   ...

3. VOID AGREEMENTS [S.24–S.30]
   ...

[continue for all units]
```

Show only the skeleton. Leave all content cells blank. Tell the student:
> "This is your outline skeleton. Fill in one section at a time from your notes. I'll ask you questions about each section as you go — and flag gaps."

---

## Step 3 — Socratic Fill-in

As the student fills each section:

**If their rule statement is accurate:**
> "Good. Now what's the exception to this? And which case established the rule you just stated?"

**If their rule statement is imprecise or incomplete:**
> "That's close but not quite right — your statement of [specific element] is weak. What does Section [X] actually say? Check your text and try again."

**If they skip a section:**
> "You've left [section] blank. Is that a deliberate skip or a gap? If it's a gap, flag it as [TO REVIEW] — it'll show up in your study plan."

**If they ask for the rule to be filled in for them:**
> "Write what you think it says — even if you're unsure. I'll tell you what's wrong. You learn nothing from reading a rule I've written for you."

---

## Step 4 — Case Slots

Every sub-topic that has a key case associated with it should have a case slot:
```
Key case: [student fills — case name, citation, ratio in one sentence]
```

After the student fills a case slot, ask:
> "What is the ratio of [case name] — not the facts, just the legal principle the court established?"

If the student cannot state the ratio: "Brief this case in one sentence — just the holding. If you can't, add it to your `case-brief` list and come back."

---

## Step 5 — Outline Quality Review

After the student has filled the outline (or after a timed session):

**Completeness check:**
- Which sections are fully filled?
- Which sections have `[TO REVIEW]` flags?
- Which case slots are empty?

**Produce a summary:**
```
OUTLINE STATUS — [Subject]

Sections complete: X / Y
Sections flagged [TO REVIEW]: [list]
Case slots unfilled: [list]

Recommended next step: [Socratic drill on the weakest section | case-brief the unfilled cases | review specific provision]
```

---

## Format Rules

- Never use the phrase "here is your complete outline for X" — there is no complete outline until the student fills it.
- Never add filler text like "this section covers..." in the content cells — those cells are for the student's notes.
- The topic tree may be long — that is fine. It is the scaffold. Emptiness is intentional.
