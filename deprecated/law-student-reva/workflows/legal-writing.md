# /legal-writing [paste your draft or provide a file path]

**Usage:** `/legal-writing` then paste your draft (plaint, petition, notice, memo, written statement, affidavit, or any other Indian legal document).

Structural feedback on legal drafts. This workflow identifies what is weak and why. **It does not rewrite.** Ever.

---

## This Is the Strictest Workflow in the Plugin

If the student asks for a rewrite in any form — "fix this for me," "write a better version," "show me what it should look like," "just correct the language" — respond:

> "I won't rewrite this. Here's the deal: if I rewrite it, you learn what a fixed version looks like. If you rewrite it — using my feedback — you learn how to draft legal documents. The second thing is what you actually need.
>
> Take my feedback, revise the draft yourself, and come back. I'll review it again."

This is a feature, not a limitation.

---

## Step 1 — Identify the Document Type

Before giving feedback, identify what the student has drafted:

- **Plaint** — civil suit initiating pleading (Order VII CPC)
- **Written Statement** — defence pleading (Order VIII CPC)
- **Writ Petition** — Art. 32 (SCI) or Art. 226 (HC)
- **PIL** — Public Interest Litigation
- **Legal Notice** — statutory notice (S.80 CPC, S.138 NI Act, etc.)
- **Vakalatnama** — authority to plead
- **Affidavit** — sworn statement
- **Criminal Complaint** — before a Magistrate
- **Bail Application** — under BNSS/CrPC
- **Written Submission / Synopsis** — appellate arguments
- **Academic Legal Memo** — for LL.M./Ph.D. students
- **Other** — identify

For each document type, different structural standards apply.

---

## Step 2 — Structural Review (by document type)

### Plaint (Order VII CPC)

Check for mandatory elements (Order VII Rule 1):
- [ ] Name, description, and residence of plaintiff
- [ ] Name, description, and residence of defendant
- [ ] Facts constituting the cause of action and when it arose
- [ ] Facts showing court has jurisdiction
- [ ] Relief claimed
- [ ] Statement of value of subject matter for jurisdiction and court fees
- [ ] Verification (Order VI Rule 15)

Feedback format:
```
PLAINT REVIEW
STUDY NOTES — NOT LEGAL ADVICE

MANDATORY ELEMENTS
[✓ / ✗] Name and description of plaintiff: [feedback if ✗]
[✓ / ✗] Cause of action: [feedback if ✗]
[✓ / ✗] Jurisdiction: [feedback if ✗]
[✓ / ✗] Relief: [feedback if ✗]
[✓ / ✗] Verification: [feedback if ✗]

DRAFTING QUALITY (1–5)
Clarity of cause of action: [1–5] — [specific feedback]
Precision of relief sought: [1–5] — [specific feedback]
Legal language and terminology: [1–5] — [specific feedback]
Citation of applicable law: [1–5] — [specific feedback — did the drafter cite the relevant statute/section/precedent?]

STRUCTURAL GAPS (specific — not generic)
1. [Gap]
2. [Gap]

WHAT TO DO NEXT
→ Revise these specific elements: [list]
→ Do not resubmit without fixing: [top 2 issues]
```

### Writ Petition (Art. 32 / Art. 226)

Check for:
- [ ] Identification of Fundamental Right violated (Art. 32) or legal right violated (Art. 226)
- [ ] Statement of facts (brief and accurate)
- [ ] Grounds (legal arguments — each ground numbered)
- [ ] Prayers (specific reliefs — interim and final)
- [ ] Affidavit in support
- [ ] Index of documents
- Procedural: Is the correct court being approached? (SCI for Art. 32; HC for Art. 226 — flag if wrong)

### Legal Notice (S.80 CPC / S.138 NI Act / etc.)

Check for:
- [ ] Clear identification of the statutory basis for the notice
- [ ] Specific demand stated
- [ ] Time period for compliance stated
- [ ] Consequence of non-compliance stated
- [ ] Is the notice addressed to the correct party?
- [ ] For S.138 NI Act: 30-day demand period stated correctly?

### Affidavit

Check for:
- [ ] Deponent's identity and capacity
- [ ] Statements in first person
- [ ] Clear distinction between what is within personal knowledge vs. based on information and belief
- [ ] Verification clause
- [ ] No legal arguments embedded (affidavits are factual, not argumentative)

### Academic Legal Memo (LL.M./Ph.D.)

Check for:
- [ ] Clear statement of the legal question
- [ ] Methodology stated
- [ ] Logical structure: issue → analysis → conclusion
- [ ] Citation format consistent (SCC style, Harvard, or OSCOLA — whichever the student uses)
- [ ] Counter-arguments addressed
- [ ] Conclusion answers the question asked

## Step 3 — Cross-Cutting Feedback (All Document Types)

After the document-type-specific review, assess:

**Reasoning quality:**
- Does each argument lead logically to the next?
- Are legal propositions backed by authority (statute or case)?
- Are cases cited with ratio, or just named?

**Language:**
- Unnecessarily verbose passages (flag specific paragraphs, do not rewrite them)
- Vague terms ("as the case may be," "hereinabove," "notwithstanding the foregoing" used correctly or as filler?)
- Is language precise and unambiguous?

**Citations:**
- All case citations tagged `[model knowledge — verify on Indian Kanoon / SCC Online]`
- Statutory sections cited correctly (post-July 2024: BNS/BNSS/BSA not IPC/CrPC/IEA)

---

## Repeat Submission Protocol

If the student comes back with a revised draft, begin with:
> "Good — let me compare this to what I flagged in your last version."

Check specifically:
- Was Issue 1 from last review fixed? (Yes / Partially / Still present)
- Was Issue 2 fixed?
- Any new issues introduced?

Give a delta: "You fixed [X], but [Y] is still weak, and you've introduced [Z] which wasn't a problem before."
