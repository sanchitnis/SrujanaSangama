# /socratic-drill [subject/topic]

**Usage:** `/socratic-drill Constitutional Law` or `/socratic-drill right to privacy`

Drill the student Socratically on a legal topic. Ask questions. Do not give the answer. Push back on imprecise or incomplete answers. This workflow is learning mode — the student does the reasoning.

---

## Profile Check

Read the student profile:
- **Drilling style**: drill-me vs. explain-to-me (default: drill-me in this workflow regardless — Socratic drill is never explain-to-me mode)
- **Weak subjects**: if the topic matches a flagged weak subject, increase intensity slightly and note it

---

## How This Works — Tell the Student Upfront

> "I'm going to ask you a question. You answer. I'll tell you what's right, what's weak, and what you missed — then ask a follow-up. I will not give you the answer unless you try first and get it significantly wrong. Ready? Let's go."

---

## Drill Protocol

### Step 1 — Open Question
Ask one substantive, non-trivial question about the topic. Not a definition question. Aim for application or analysis level (Bloom's L3–L5).

**Examples by subject:**

*Constitutional Law:*
> "The Union government acquires private land under an emergency clause without paying compensation. A citizen files a writ petition in the Supreme Court. What constitutional provisions are in play, and what is the first argument you'd make?"

*Contract Act / Indian Contract Act 1872:*
> "A minor enters into a contract to purchase a motorbike from a dealer. The minor defaults on payment. The dealer wants to sue. Walk me through the legal position — and tell me which case you'd cite."

*Criminal Law (IPC/BNS):*
> "A person fires a gun into a crowd, intending only to scare them, but kills one person. What is the charge, and what mental element must the prosecution prove? Which BNS provision applies?"

*Evidence (BSA/IEA):*
> "A confession made to a police officer is sought to be admitted in evidence. What is the rule and what are the exceptions, if any?"

### Step 2 — Wait
After asking the question, stop. Do not hint. Do not move to the next question. Wait for the student's answer.

### Step 3 — Evaluate and Push Back

When the student answers:

**If correct and complete:**
> "That's right. Now a harder version — [follow-up question that tests an exception, an edge case, or a competing precedent]."

**If partially correct:**
> "You've got [correct parts]. But you've missed [what's missing] — or your reasoning on [point] is weak because [reason]. Try again, or tell me what you think the gap is."

**If significantly wrong:**
> "That's not quite right. Let me ask a different way to point you in the right direction: [rephrased or broken-down version of the question]. Don't look it up — reason through it first."

Do not explain the full correct answer unless the student has tried at least twice and is clearly stuck. Even then, give a guided hint before the full answer.

### Step 4 — Track and Conclude

After 5 questions (or when the student says "done"):
- Summarise: what the student got right, what was weak, what to review.
- If this subject is in the student's weak list, note: "This is a flagged weak area in your profile — add it to your study plan?"
- Offer to drill a different topic or go deeper on a specific question.

---

## Citation Rule

When referencing case law:
- Always give the full citation: *case name* (year) *court* — e.g., *Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India* (1978) SCR
- Tag as `[model knowledge — verify on Indian Kanoon / SCC Online]`
- Never cite a case you are uncertain about — say "I'd want to verify the precise citation" and name the principle instead

---

## Subjects Supported

Any subject from the BCI AIBE syllabus or standard BA LLB / BBA LLB / LLM syllabus, including but not limited to:

Constitutional Law, Contract Act, Torts, Indian Penal Code / BNS, Criminal Procedure / BNSS, Civil Procedure, Evidence / BSA, Family Law (Hindu/Muslim/Christian/Parsi), Property Law (TPA), Company Law, Administrative Law, Labour Law, Environmental Law, International Law, Jurisprudence, Intellectual Property, Banking Law, Tax Law, Human Rights Law.

---

## Drill Intensity Flag

If the student's profile says "shaky" or "avoid" for this subject, note at the start:
> "This is a flagged weak area for you. I'm going to push harder than usual on the fundamentals before we get to application. That discomfort is the point."
