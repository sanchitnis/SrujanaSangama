# Session Type: Synopsis Writing & Colloquium Preparation

**Purpose:** Guide the scholar through drafting all 5 sections of the PhD synopsis and prepare them for the pre-registration colloquium presentation and Q&A.

**Estimated total time:** 45–90 minutes (can be split across sessions)

---

## Phase 1 — Readiness Check (5 min)

Prerequisites for starting a synopsis:
- CWEE completed (coursework exit exam passed)
- Research topic confirmed with guide
- At least 10–15 papers read in the domain

Ask:
1. *"Has your guide confirmed the research topic?"*
2. *"Have you completed your coursework (CWEE)?"*
3. *"Have you done any initial literature reading?"*

If prerequisites are not met: recommend completing them first. Offer to help with the remaining prerequisite rather than the synopsis.

---

## Phase 2 — Synopsis Drafting (45–60 min)

Activate `synopsis-builder.md`. Work through sections one at a time.

### Section 1 — Background & Motivation (10 min)
- Ask the scholar for their 3 key papers establishing the state of the art
- Help identify the gap: *"What did these papers NOT solve?"*
- Draft a 2-sentence gap statement

### Section 2 — Research Objectives (10 min)
- Collect the scholar's draft objectives
- Sharpen each: is it specific? measurable? achievable in 3–4 years?
- Final output: 3–5 numbered research objectives

### Section 3 — Hypotheses (5 min)
- For empirical research: formulate H0 + H1 for each key objective
- For design science: define the design claims instead
- For survey-based: skip formal hypotheses; describe expected findings

### Section 4 — Methodology (10 min)
- Select methodology type (empirical / design science / survey / case study / mixed)
- Describe the research phases (data collection, analysis, tool development, evaluation)
- Define the validation approach

### Section 5 — Contributions & Timeline (10 min)
- List 3–5 specific, novel contributions (not literature reviews)
- Draft a preliminary timeline table from coursework through thesis submission

---

## Phase 3 — Colloquium Presentation Coaching (20 min)

Structure the 15–20 minute presentation (per `synopsis-builder.md` protocol):
1. Problem statement (3 min) — sharpen wording
2. Literature gap (3 min) — 3 papers + clear gap
3. Objectives and hypotheses (3 min) — numbered list
4. Methodology (4 min) — diagram if possible
5. Contributions and timeline (3 min)

Work through each section with the scholar. Identify the 2 weakest sections. Spend extra time on them.

---

## Phase 4 — Mock Q&A (15 min)

Run the standard mock questions (from `synopsis-builder.md`):
1. *"How is your research different from [specific paper]?"*
2. *"Why is your methodology the right one for this problem?"*
3. *"What will you do if your primary data source is unavailable?"*
4. *"How many publications do you expect and in which journals?"*
5. *"What is your single most important contribution?"*

For each answer: score the clarity (1–3 scale) and suggest improvements.
