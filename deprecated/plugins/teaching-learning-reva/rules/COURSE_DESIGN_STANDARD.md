# REVA University AI System Instructions — Course Design Quality Standard

This document defines the strict quality guidelines for course design, syllabus structures, and outcome-based education (OBE) metrics at REVA University. The AI agent acts as a co-designer and quality auditor alongside faculty members to ensure compliance before any course syllabus is baselined.

---

## 1. raised PEO & CO Ambition in the AI Era

With AI co-working integration, graduates are expected to operate at higher cognitive and functional levels from day one. Program Educational Objectives (PEOs) and Course Outcomes (COs) must raise their cognitive floor.

*   **Assumption**: Students have access to generative AI assistants as standard accelerators.
*   **Target**: Outcomes must emphasize **synthesis, design, judgment, critical evaluation, and ethical reasoning** (which AI cannot perform independently).
*   **Drafting Principle**: COs must avoid generic verbs like "understand" or "know" and instead employ action-oriented verbs mapped to Bloom's Cognitive Levels.

---

## 2. Bloom's Taxonomy & HOTS Dominance ($\ge 60\%$)

Every syllabus must show a clear dominance of Higher-Order Thinking Skills (HOTS) across its learning units and session designs.

*   **HOTS Cognitive Levels**:
    *   **Level 4 (Analyze)**: Differentiate, organize, relate, compare, research, audit.
    *   **Level 5 (Evaluate)**: Appraise, argue, defend, judge, support, critique.
    *   **Level 6 (Create)**: Design, assemble, construct, invent, formulate, author, develop.
*   **Standard**: At least **60%** of the course content, assessment weight, and outcomes must map directly to Levels 4, 5, or 6.
*   **Active Learning Mapping**: Every unit must explicitly list active learning strategies (e.g., Flipped Classroom, Think-Pair-Share, Socratic Seminars) mapped to these HOTS targets.

---

## 3. CO-PO & PSO Strength Mapping

Each course outline must include an explicit, justified mapping of COs to the 12 NBA Program Outcomes (POs) and specific Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs).

*   **Strength Levels**:
    *   **1 (Slight/Low)**: Indirect or minor reinforcement.
    *   **2 (Moderate/Medium)**: Explicitly assessed in projects, homework, or labs.
    *   **3 (Substantial/High)**: Direct, central focus of the course; measured as a core outcome.
*   **Justification**: General maps are unacceptable. Every mapping must have a brief, traceable justification linking the outcome to course learning activities.

---

## 4. Portfolio-First Linkage

A course is not complete without practical, public-facing proof of competence. 

*   **Rule**: Every course must link its final outcomes to a tangible portfolio output:
    *   A peer-reviewed publication or technical draft.
    *   A functional product, software system, or open-source contribution.
    *   An experiential service-learning report.
*   The portfolio milestone must be integrated directly into the Continuous Internal Assessment (CIA) cycle.

---

## 5. Liberal Studies, Sustainability, and IKS Integration

Consistent with REVA University's Vision and Mission, the course syllabus must explicitly integrate:
*   **Liberal Studies / Creative Disciplines**: Connections to arts, design thinking, or literature.
*   **Sustainability & Values**: Social responsibility and environmental impact indicators.
*   **Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS)**: Contextual Indian case studies, historical Indian engineering achievements, and ethical principles of stewardship.
