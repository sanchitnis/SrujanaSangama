### **Phase 1: Ideation & Validation**
*This phase ensures you're solving a real problem for a real audience. It fits well within introductory software engineering or human-computer interaction courses.*

---

#### **Assignment 1: Problem & Market Analysis Report**
*   **Corresponding Steps:** 1 (Identify Problem), 2 (Brainstorm Solutions), 3 (Market Research)
*   **CS Course Integration:** Introduction to Software Engineering, Professional Ethics in CS
*   **Description:** Students will identify a specific problem that can be solved with technology. They must research and analyze at least three existing competitors or alternative solutions, detailing their technical approaches, strengths, and weaknesses. The goal is to define a unique market gap.
*   **Deliverable:** A 5-7 page **Market Analysis Report** including:
    *   A clear Problem Statement.
    *   A Competitive Analysis Matrix comparing existing solutions on features, technology stack, and pricing.
    *   A summary of the identified market opportunity and unique value proposition.

#### **Assignment 2: Customer Discovery & Lean Canvas**
*   **Corresponding Steps:** 4 (Target Audience), 5 (Customer Interviews), 6 (Value Proposition), 7 (Lean Canvas)
*   **CS Course Integration:** Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Software Product Management
*   **Description:** Students must conduct structured interviews with at least 5-10 potential users who fit their target audience profile. The focus is on validating the problem, not pitching a solution. Based on the insights, they will refine their value proposition and map it onto a Lean Canvas.
*   **Deliverable:** A **Presentation Deck (10-12 slides)** containing:
    *   2-3 detailed User Personas.
    *   A summary of key insights and direct quotes from the interviews.
    *   A completed Lean Canvas, with each section justified by interview data.

---

### **Phase 2: Planning & Foundation**
*This phase translates the validated idea into a concrete project plan, covering the technical and business foundations.*

---

#### **Assignment 3: Project Identity & Technical Stack Proposal**
*   **Corresponding Steps:** 8 (Name/Domain), 11 (Co-founder/Team), 12 (Branding)
*   **CS Course Integration:** Web Development, Software Project Management
*   **Description:** Students will choose a name, design a simple logo, and write a mission statement. Critically, they must then propose and justify a complete technical stack (e.g., language, framework, database, cloud provider) for their future MVP, explaining why these choices are appropriate for the project's scope and scale.
*   **Deliverable:** A **Project Charter Document** that includes:
    *   Project Name, Mission Statement, and basic branding assets (logo, color palette).
    *   A "Team Skills & Roles" section outlining the required expertise.
    *   A detailed Technical Stack Proposal with a justification for each technology choice.

#### **Assignment 4: Financial & Legal Structure Analysis**
*   **Corresponding Steps:** 9 (Financial Model), 10 (Legal Structure)
*   **CS Course Integration:** Capstone Project Prep, Special Topics: Tech Entrepreneurship
*   **Description:** Students will create a 3-year financial projection for their startup. This model must include estimated operational costs (e.g., server hosting, API fees, marketing) and a revenue model. They will also write a brief analysis comparing two common legal structures (e.g., LLC vs. C-Corp) for a tech startup.
*   **Deliverables:**
    1.  A **Financial Model Spreadsheet** (e.g., Excel, Google Sheets) with detailed projections and a list of all assumptions.
    2.  A 2-page **Legal Structure Analysis Report** recommending a structure and explaining the rationale.

---

### **Phase 3: Product Development & Testing**
*This is the core technical phase where students build, test, and refine their product. These assignments are perfect for capstone or advanced development courses.*

---

#### **Assignment 5: MVP Specification & System Design**
*   **Corresponding Steps:** 13 (Define MVP), 14 (Wireframes/Mockups)
*   **CS Course Integration:** Software Engineering, Systems Design
*   **Description:** Students will write a formal specification for their Minimum Viable Product (MVP), detailing the core user stories and features. They will then design the system architecture, creating wireframes for the UI, a database schema, and an API specification.
*   **Deliverable:** A **System Design Document** containing:
    *   A prioritized list of MVP features and user stories.
    *   UI/UX wireframes for all primary screens.
    *   A database schema diagram.
    *   A RESTful API specification (endpoints, request/response formats).

#### **Assignment 6: MVP Implementation & Deployment**
*   **Corresponding Steps:** 15 (Build MVP), 16 (Internal Testing)
*   **CS Course Integration:** Capstone Project, Full-Stack Development, Mobile App Development
*   **Description:** This is the main coding assignment. Students will build the functional MVP based on their design document from the previous assignment. A critical requirement is to deploy the application to a publicly accessible cloud environment (e.g., Vercel, Heroku, AWS, Azure).
*   **Deliverables:**
    1.  A link to the **live, deployed MVP**.
    2.  A link to the **source code repository** (e.g., GitHub) with a clear README file.

#### **Assignment 7: Beta Testing & Iteration Report**
*   **Corresponding Steps:** 17 (Closed Beta), 18 (Gather Feedback), 19 (Iterate)
*   **CS Course Integration:** Software Quality Assurance, Capstone Project
*   **Description:** Students will recruit at least 10 beta testers to use their live MVP. They must design a feedback survey or conduct short user testing sessions to collect qualitative and quantitative data. Based on the analysis, they will identify bugs and prioritize features for the next version.
*   **Deliverable:** A **Beta Test Report** including:
    *   The methodology used to collect feedback (e.g., survey questions, testing script).
    *   A summary of findings, including a list of identified bugs and key user suggestions.
    *   A prioritized backlog (e.g., as a Trello board or GitHub Issues) for the next development sprint.
