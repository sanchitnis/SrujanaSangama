# SrujanaSangama: A Collaborative Agentic Workplace for the REVA Community

> A centralized private intelligence platform for all T.R.A.C.K. activities — **Teaching & Learning, Research, Academic Administration, Consulting & Product Development, and Kaizen & Institutional Excellence** — serving faculty, UG/PG students, research scholars, administrative departments, and institutional leadership of REVA University at individual, team, centre, school, university, and strategic levels.

SrujanaSangama is a **Human-AI Collaborative System** aligned to ADDIE, NBA-OBE, NEP 2020, and REVA's Vision and Mission. It ensures that AI augments human judgment, institutional wisdom, and faculty expertise — never replacing them. The platform is designed so that the quality and productivity of every work item across all university functions improves measurably, while every individual in the REVA community continues to grow as a human being.

A parallel platform, **SrujanaBuddy**, serves the student community with equivalent agentic support for their learning, Srujana pathway, and personal growth. The two platforms share institutional data and complement each other as parts of one coherent REVA agentic ecosystem.

## Foundational Design Philosophy

### Human-AI Collaboration as the Operating Principle

SrujanaSangama is not an AI system that generates academic content autonomously. It is a **collaborative intelligence environment** where human judgment, institutional wisdom, faculty expertise, industry insight, and student agency are the primary authors — and AI is the accelerator, the quality mirror, and the tireless first-draft partner.

Every step — analysis, curriculum design, industry validation, BOS deliberation, content creation, assessment design, student mentoring — is a **human-AI dialogue**: AI contributes speed, breadth, pattern recognition, and structured outputs; humans contribute values, contextual judgment, relationships, creativity, and final authority.

No curriculum decision, no course approval, no assessment goes forward on AI output alone. The platform is a scaffold for better human decisions, not a replacement for them.

### The Enterprising Graduate as the North Star

The central output of this platform is a graduate who is **enterprising** — someone who sees problems as opportunities, initiates action, collaborates across boundaries, communicates with clarity, reasons ethically under uncertainty, adapts continuously, and creates value through technology, service, art, research, or leadership.

This is broader than entrepreneurship. An enterprising nurse, civil servant, researcher, and founder all share these qualities. Every course, every assessment, and every Srujana stage is designed with this graduate in mind.

### PEO Ambition in the AI Era

Program Educational Objectives are **raised** in this platform. AI augmentation means REVA graduates are expected to perform tasks and take on responsibilities that previously required 5–6 years of industry experience. COs embed the assumption that students work alongside AI tools — assessed on judgment, synthesis, creative application, and ethical reasoning, not on tasks that AI can do faster.

## Platform Promise

SrujanaSangama is designed so that:

* Every faculty member spends more time on work that requires human judgment, creativity, and relationships — and less on tasks that AI can do faster.
* Every work product — course, research paper, grant proposal, accreditation document, consulting brief — is of higher quality because AI provides a brainstorming platform, a structured first draft, a quality mirror in terms of critical review,  and a governance check that humans then own and improve.
* The institution continuously improves — in teaching quality, research output, accreditation readiness, NIRF position, and the enterprising outcomes of its graduates.

## Domain 1 — Teaching \& Learning

Faculty are the primary architects of the student learning experience. Every task in this domain is a human-AI collaboration: AI drafts, structures, and audits; faculty decide, contextualise, and deliver.

### What Faculty Do — and What the System Enables

**Curriculum \& Programme Design**

Faculty design programmes that prepare graduates for AI-era careers. The system enables them to:

* Scan the future of jobs landscape — WEF, NASSCOM, LinkedIn, global education benchmarks — and synthesise it into a structured briefing for curriculum committee deliberation
* Design Programme Educational Objectives raised to AI-era ambition, where graduates are expected to work alongside AI and perform at levels that previously required years of industry experience
* Formulate Course Outcomes that embed AI augmentation assumptions: assessments focus on judgment, synthesis, creative application, and ethical reasoning, not on tasks AI can do faster
* Map COs to POs and PSOs with strength levels, ensuring every programme outcome is addressed and every CO is traceable
* Design programme structures with credit distribution, semester layout, theory-lab-project balance, and Srujana stage integration aligned to REVA mission
* Sequence prerequisites and design course dependencies with visual dependency graphs
* Design all four models of minors: intra-REVA interdisciplinary, NPTEL/SWAYAM credit-bearing, industry micro-credential, and international joint minors
* Design UG-PG integrated pathways (3+1+1 and 4+1) under UGC 2022 regulations
* Embed liberal arts, performing arts, literature, sports, and sustainability into programme structures per REVA Mission
* Prepare BOS-ready proposals with regulatory compliance checks against UGC, AICTE, and NEP 2020 before submission

**Session Planning \& Content Development**

Faculty design and deliver sessions that develop genuinely capable graduates. The system enables them to:

* Generate first-draft session plans with active learning strategies, Bloom's-level alignment, and Srujana stage indicators for faculty selection and adaptation
* Develop content objects — lecture notes, slide decks, case studies, problem sets, reading lists, video scripts — as first drafts for faculty review, contextualisation, and approval
* Design flipped classroom, project-based learning, inquiry-based, and collaborative learning structures
* Embed Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS), universal values, and wellbeing dimensions into course content per REVA Vision
* Produce REVA-branded, OBE-compliant course syllabi for publication
* Configure LMS course structures, H5P interactives, and digital assessment tools under faculty and IT direction

**Assessment \& Evaluation**

Faculty design assessments that verify genuine student capability in an AI era. The system enables them to:

* Audit every assignment for AI-triviality: tasks completable by AI without student judgment are flagged for redesign
* Design Higher Order Thinking (HOTS) assignments that require analysis, evaluation, and creation — with creative latitude, real-world relevance, and student choice
* Design proctored in-person activities that verify genuine brain-and-hand competency
* Build question banks tagged by CO, Bloom's level, difficulty, and AI-augmentation assumption
* Design rubrics — analytic and holistic — for assignments, projects, presentations, and portfolio review
* Plan CIA calendars with component weights, CO mapping, and portfolio milestone alignment
* Compute direct and indirect CO attainment, identify at-risk students, and generate remediation plans for faculty and programme committee review
* Design viva voce instruments and peer assessment structures

**Portfolio-First Pedagogy**

Every course in SrujanaSangama is designed to produce a tangible artefact — a publication, product, open source contribution, or documented solution. The system enables faculty to:

* Link every CO to a portfolio artefact type and define milestone timelines
* Design portfolio rubrics for artefact quality, originality, and public impact
* Scaffold the course project → writing → review → conference/journal submission pipeline
* Map portfolio artefacts to placement readiness and recruiter-facing profiles
* Audit portfolio integrity: artefacts represent genuine student work with verifiable authorship and declared AI use

**Student Mentoring \& Personalised Pathways**

Faculty advise students across diverse learning needs. The system enables them to:

* Identify at-risk students early and receive data-driven insights on CO performance, Srujana readiness, and placement gaps — with faculty acting on the signals
* Generate personalised pathways with faculty advisors co-signing the plan alongside the student
* Accelerate fast learners through honours tracks and early access to Srujana Stage 3
* Support slow learners through AI tutoring, structured remediation, and paced re-learning with faculty check-ins
* Counsel students on Srujana stage readiness, elective and certification choices, and internship tracks
* Track cohort-level Srujana progress across all four stages

**The Srujana Pathway**

The Srujana pathway is the student's personal narrative of growth from learner to creator, supported by SrujanaBuddy on the student side and by faculty mentors through SrujanaSangama.

|Stage|Name|What Happens|
|-|-|-|
|**Stage 1**|Skill Formation|Structured skill development through courses, labs, and certifications; foundational portfolio artefacts (Semesters 1–4)|
|**Stage 2**|Real World Immersion|Structured industry or research internship with COs, mentor mapping, and reflective portfolio (Semesters 3–6)|
|**Stage 3**|Create and Contribute|Product development, research project, patent filing, open source contribution, or published paper (Semesters 5–7)|
|**Stage 4**|Enterprise|Venture design through REVA NEST; pitch preparation, business model, legal setup, investor readiness (Semesters 7–8 and beyond)|

Faculty mentors use SrujanaSangama to track each student's stage, recommend electives and certifications, review portfolio milestones, connect students to industry mentors, and support transition between stages.

**Faculty Development**

Faculty are lifelong learners. The system enables them to:

* Identify pedagogy gaps and generate coaching plans for faculty mentor review and ownership
* Design FDP programmes aligned to OBE, active learning, AI-era pedagogy, and portfolio-first principles
* Facilitate micro-teaching observations with structured debrief guides
* Connect faculty research to course content and student projects (research-teaching nexus)
* Guide faculty in designing AI-era courses: raising PEO ambition, redesigning assessments, embedding AI literacy

## Domain 2 — Research

Faculty researchers carry a full research lifecycle — from idea through funding, execution, dissemination, and impact. AI accelerates and structures each stage; the faculty member retains intellectual ownership and institutional accountability.

### What Faculty Do — and What the System Enables

**Research Identity \& Competency**

Faculty build and maintain a credible research identity. The system enables them to:

* Build and maintain research profiles across ORCID, Scopus, and Google Scholar with structured data
* Map their research interests to SDGs, national priority areas, and institutional research themes
* Identify competency gaps and generate personal learning roadmaps for research skill development
* Build a personal academic brand — communications, conference presence, media engagement — for faculty adaptation and ownership
* Identify Ikigai alignment between personal strengths, research passions, institutional needs, and fundable areas

**Opportunity Scanning \& Collaboration**

Research opportunity is too often missed for want of structured scanning. The system enables faculty to:

* Continuously scan active funding opportunities across DST, SERB, DBT, ICMR, ICSSR, UGC, Fulbright, and industry bodies — synthesised into a structured briefing for faculty action
* Map interdisciplinary collaboration opportunities within REVA across schools, departments, and research centres
* Identify potential co-investigators and international research partners by competency and research alignment
* Map SDG impact of ongoing and planned research

**Grant Writing \& Funding**

Winning grants requires disciplined preparation. The system enables faculty to:

* Profile target funding agencies and their priority areas, review criteria, and scoring rubrics
* Generate structured first-draft grant proposals (SERB, DST, DBT, ICMR, UGC, industry) for faculty authorship, review, and submission
* Draft budgets, timelines, and work plans aligned to agency requirements
* Score proposals against agency criteria and surface gaps before submission
* Track revision cycles and resubmission strategies

**Research Execution \& Methodology**

Faculty manage active research projects with complex timelines and outputs. The system enables them to:

* Design research methodologies and experiment or study structures for faculty validation
* Maintain research diaries, milestone logs, and progress documentation
* Track research project milestones and surface delays early
* Support data collection planning and analysis frameworks

**Publication Pipeline**

Publishing requires sustained effort across multiple stages. The system enables faculty to:

* Conduct structured literature reviews with citation network mapping
* Generate first-draft manuscript structures for faculty writing, editing, and ownership
* Target appropriate journals by impact factor, scope, quartile, and turnaround time
* Review manuscripts for scholarly writing standards, research ethics, and publication formatting
* Prepare conference papers, rebuttals, and revision responses
* Manage preprint submissions and track post-submission progress

**Patent \& IP Development**

Indian patent filing is a complex, multi-stage process. The system guides faculty through:

* Invention identification and disclosure documentation
* Patentability assessment against Indian Patent Act criteria
* Prior art search simulation across major patent databases
* Draft preparation of Form 1, Form 2, and Form 3 for faculty and legal review
* Patent prosecution support and commercialisation strategy scoping
* Technology Readiness Level (TRL) assessment for research-to-product transitions

**PhD Supervision**

Guiding a research scholar is a long-duration, high-stakes responsibility. The system enables supervising faculty to:

* Onboard new scholars with structured topic selection and research planning frameworks
* Review synopses and research plans against REVA PhD regulations and statutory requirements
* Advise on coursework selection, skill development, and methodology
* Monitor milestone progress — coursework completion, synopsis submission, open seminars, pre-submission review — with early warning signals for delays
* Scaffold publication planning: target journals, co-authorship frameworks, and submission timelines
* Prepare scholars for viva voce: mock viva structure, expected examiner questions, defence preparation
* Maintain compliant documentation across the full supervision lifecycle

**Research Leadership \& Brand**

Senior faculty lead research groups and represent REVA's research standing. The system enables them to:

* Design and document research group structures, lab or centre establishment frameworks, and team onboarding plans
* Build institutional research brand communications for faculty leadership review and publication
* Mentor junior faculty researchers and provide structured guidance on career trajectory
* Track research output at individual, department, and institutional levels for NIRF and accreditation purposes

**Book \& Monograph Writing**

Long-form academic writing requires sustained scaffolding. The system supports faculty in:

* Designing book proposals, chapter outlines, and writing schedules
* Maintaining the academic writing workflow across a sustained timeline
* Targeting appropriate publishers and preparing peer review responses

## Domain 3 — Academic Administration

Academic Administration is the institutional backbone that enables every other T.R.A.C.K. domain to function. It spans ten interconnected verticals — from the moment a student enquires about REVA to the day they become an engaged alumnus. Faculty, administrative staff, and institutional leaders all operate within these verticals, each carrying governance, compliance, reporting, and operational responsibilities that determine REVA's quality, ranking, and reputation. The system ensures these responsibilities are met efficiently and to a high standard, so people spend less time on paperwork and more on judgment, relationships, and improvement.

### What Faculty and Administrators Do — and What the System Enables

**Vertical 1 — Admissions & Branding**

REVA's reputation and enrolment quality begin with how the institution presents itself and engages prospective students. The system enables admissions teams and leadership to:

* Generate compelling, accurate programme brochures, digital content, and campaign materials aligned to REVA brand standards and updated with current placement, research, and accreditation achievements
* Build and maintain a structured prospective student enquiry database with follow-up workflows, communication templates, and conversion tracking
* Analyse application trends, source channel effectiveness, and student quality distribution to inform recruitment strategy
* Produce competitive positioning analysis comparing REVA programmes against peer institutions by ranking, accreditation, placement, and specialisation — for leadership review and marketing planning
* Track state-wise, district-wise, and school-category-wise enrolment patterns to identify outreach priorities
* Draft counselling scripts, FAQ documents, and virtual open-house materials for admissions officers
* Maintain regulatory compliance documentation for fee structures, reservation categories, and admission process transparency as required by UGC, AICTE, and state regulatory bodies
* Monitor and respond to student and parent feedback during the admissions cycle to improve conversion and experience

**Vertical 2 — Academics & Skill Enhancement**

This is the core of REVA's academic quality machinery — the vertical that keeps programmes current, outcomes measurable, governance sound, and graduates competitive. The system enables faculty and academic administrators to:

*Curriculum & OBE Governance*
* Maintain CO-PO matrices with current strength levels and evidence across all programmes
* Compute direct CO attainment from CIA and end-semester scores and indirect attainment from surveys and portfolio review
* Define and review attainment thresholds for each CO, and generate structured remediation plans for students below threshold
* Generate course-end reports, CO attainment summaries, and programme-level heatmaps for committee review
* Format curriculum change proposals to UGC/AICTE regulatory standards before BOS submission
* Run automated pre-submission compliance checks against UGC, AICTE, NEP 2020, and REVA internal policy
* Generate BOS meeting agendas, briefing documents, and draft minutes for secretary review and sign-off
* Track all curriculum changes through the full cycle: proposal → industry validation → BOS deliberation → approval → implementation → evaluation

*Accreditation & Ranking*
* Generate criterion-wise SAR documentation with evidence links and self-assessment scoring for NBA and NAAC peer committee review
* Prepare NAAC DVV documentation and metric data with supporting evidence
* Compile full OBE audit trails for NBA inspection readiness and conduct pre-visit compliance audits
* Analyse REVA's current position on each NIRF parameter with priority gap identification and measurable improvement targets
* Automate NIRF data collection, cleaning, and submission-ready packaging across all contributing units
* Track research output, placement quality, admissions excellence, and faculty development as continuous NIRF inputs

*Regulatory Compliance*
* Document academic programme approvals with full regulatory evidence trails
* Track statutory council-specific obligations across AICTE, Council of Architecture, Bar Council of India, and other bodies
* Monitor fee regulation compliance, autonomy and affiliation governance, and academic calendar adherence

*Skill Enhancement*
* Track student skill development across certifications, NPTEL/SWAYAM courses, industry micro-credentials, and professional assessments
* Map skill attainment to programme COs and to NASSCOM, WEF, and industry skill frameworks
* Generate cohort-level skill gap reports for programme committees and placement teams
* Design and coordinate skill enhancement programmes — boot camps, certification drives, workshops — aligned to placement and industry readiness requirements

**Vertical 3 — Examination & Evaluation**

Examinations are a high-stakes, high-volume operation where quality, fairness, and integrity are non-negotiable. The system enables examination cells, faculty, and academic administrators to:

* Generate balanced, OBE-compliant question papers with CO coverage matrices, Bloom's level distribution, and AI-readiness audit flags — for faculty review and approval before submission
* Manage question paper submission workflows with version control, confidentiality protocols, and deadline tracking
* Coordinate CIA scheduling, mark entry, and consolidation with automated consistency checks and error flags for faculty review
* Generate CIA result summaries by CO with at-risk student identification for faculty mentoring action
* Coordinate end-semester examination scheduling, hall allocation, invigilation duty assignment, and seating arrangement documentation
* Manage answer script distribution, evaluation workflows, and moderation protocols with full audit trails
* Generate result processing packages — tabulation, grade computation, CGPA calculation — aligned to REVA regulation and UGC standards, for verification and sign-off
* Track and document revaluation, photocopy, and grievance resolution workflows with full correspondence records
* Prepare examination-related data for statutory reporting, accreditation evidence, and NIRF submissions
* Coordinate supplementary and arrear examination cycles with scheduling, eligibility checks, and result processing

**Vertical 4 — Research & Innovation Management**

Research administration is distinct from doing research — it is the institutional scaffolding that enables research to happen at scale. The system enables research cells, DST units, and institutional research offices to:

* Maintain a living, searchable inventory of all ongoing and completed research projects across the university — by faculty, school, funding agency, SDG alignment, and status
* Track research output metrics — publications, patents, citations, funded projects, doctoral completions — at faculty, department, school, and institutional levels for NIRF, accreditation, and leadership reporting
* Generate research performance dashboards for HOD, Dean, and VC review, with trend analysis and peer benchmarking
* Coordinate the institutional process for grant proposal submission: internal review, finance sign-off, legal checks, and submission documentation
* Maintain compliant records of ethics committee approvals, data sharing agreements, and regulatory clearances across all research projects
* Track patent portfolio status — filed, published, granted, licensed — and generate IP portfolio reports for institutional and NIRF purposes
* Coordinate doctoral programme administration: scholar registration, coursework records, milestone compliance, scholarship tracking, and statutory reporting to UGC and the university's doctoral board
* Support the establishment and reporting of research centres, incubation units, and funded labs — including utilisation certificates, audit support, and regulatory reporting to funding agencies
* Monitor and report on industry-sponsored research, consultancy revenue, and technology transfer activity as institutional research commercialisation indicators

**Vertical 5 — Training & Placement**

Placement is one of REVA's most visible institutional outcomes and a primary NIRF parameter. The system enables the Training & Placement Cell, faculty placement coordinators, and programme teams to:

* Maintain a structured, searchable company database with engagement history, role preferences, campus visit records, and relationship owner tracking
* Generate company engagement proposals, campus recruitment event briefs, and pre-placement talk coordination materials for TPC review
* Track the full student placement cycle: registration, eligibility, application, interview scheduling, offer, acceptance, and joining — with cohort-level dashboards for TPC and leadership
* Identify placement-readiness gaps at the individual and cohort level by mapping portfolio strength, CO attainment, and certification status to target company requirements
* Generate aptitude test, group discussion, and technical interview preparation materials calibrated to specific company profiles and role requirements
* Track internship-to-PPO (Pre-Placement Offer) conversion rates and identify institutional levers for improvement
* Produce placement analytics for NIRF data submission, accreditation evidence, and marketing communication — including CTC distribution, sector diversity, role quality, and year-on-year trend analysis
* Coordinate training interventions — communication skills, aptitude, coding, domain knowledge — aligned to placement readiness gaps and company visit calendars
* Maintain compliant documentation of all placement offers, acceptances, and employment verification records

**Vertical 6 — Alumni, Clubs & Forums**

Alumni are REVA's most enduring community and a strategic resource for mentoring, placement, funding, and institutional reputation. Student clubs and forums are the incubators of leadership, creativity, and community. The system enables alumni relations offices, student affairs teams, and faculty advisors to:

* Maintain a structured, up-to-date alumni database with career trajectory tracking, geographic distribution, and sector representation
* Generate personalised alumni engagement communications — reunion invitations, achievement spotlights, mentoring programme invitations, and research collaboration calls — at scale
* Coordinate alumni mentoring programmes for Srujana Stage 2 and Stage 3 students: mentor matching, engagement tracking, and outcomes documentation
* Track alumni contributions — guest lectures, project mentoring, internship hosting, placement referrals, and donations — for institutional reporting and relationship management
* Generate alumni achievement reports for accreditation, NIRF, and institutional brand building
* Support student club registration, annual planning, event documentation, and reporting with structured templates and workflow coordination
* Coordinate inter-collegiate events, cultural fests, and technical forums with event planning, budget tracking, participation documentation, and post-event reporting
* Maintain records of club membership, activity participation, and student leadership for co-curricular attainment tracking and placement readiness profiling

**Vertical 7 — Extra-Curricular Activities: Sports, Cultural, NSS, NCC, School Adoption**

Extra-curricular participation is not peripheral — it is integral to the enterprising graduate that REVA aims to produce. The system enables sports directors, NSS/NCC coordinators, cultural committee members, and faculty in-charges to:

* Maintain student participation records across sports, cultural events, NSS camps, NCC activities, and community outreach — in a structured, queryable format for academic record, accreditation, and NIRF reporting

Structured mentoring at REVA ensures that no student is without guidance through their academic journey. The system enables faculty mentors, advisors, and counselling teams to:

* Maintain complete, structured mentor-mentee records covering academic performance, attendance, CO attainment, Srujana stage, career goals, and intervention history
* Generate data-driven insights on each mentee: at-risk flags, performance trends, engagement signals, and placement readiness gaps — for mentor review and action
* Produce structured mentoring session agendas and post-session notes with action items and follow-up tracking
* Track remediation interventions — extra tutorials, AI tutoring assignments, peer mentoring — with outcomes for programme committee review
* Coordinate counselling referrals for students experiencing academic, personal, or wellbeing challenges — with confidential documentation protocols

**Vertical 9 — SLCM (Student Lifecycle Management)**

The Student Lifecycle Management system — REVA's University Management System — is the operational spine of student data across admission to alumni conversion. The system enables academic administrators, exam cells, finance teams, and IT to:

* Generate structured data feeds, reports, and analytics from SLCM data for accreditation, NIRF, and statutory reporting — removing the manual data extraction burden from administrators

* Support the annual statutory reporting cycle to UGC, AICTE, and state regulatory bodies by packaging SLCM data into required submission formats

**Vertical 10 — IQAC (Internal Quality Assurance Cell)**

IQAC is the institutional engine of continuous quality improvement — gathering evidence, identifying gaps, coordinating improvement actions, and maintaining the documentation that accreditors, rankers, and regulators rely on. The system enables the IQAC Coordinator, quality teams, and institutional leadership to:

* Generate Annual Quality Assurance Reports (AQARs) with structured data, narrative sections, and evidence annexures for NAAC submission — drawn from live institutional data rather than manual compilation

* Generate evidence packages for each NAAC metric and NBA criterion — linking institutional data to accreditation requirements with gap analysis for leadership review
* Maintain the institutional document repository: policies, regulations, governance records, committee minutes, and compliance evidence in a structured, searchable, audit-ready form
* Coordinate the Academic and Administrative Audit (AAA) cycle — scheduling, checklists, observation reports, and corrective action tracking across departments
* Support the preparation and follow-through of institutional review meetings — Academic Council, Board of Management, Finance Committee — with agenda preparation, briefing documents, and minutes generation for secretary review and sign-off

* Design MOU frameworks for consulting relationships with scope, milestone, and IP assignment clarity

## Domain 5 — Kaizen & Institutional Excellence

Kaizen (改善 — continuous improvement) is not a departmental function at REVA — it is a way of being. This domain covers the full spectrum from the individual faculty member's daily habits to the university's annual strategic vision. The platform treats personal wellbeing and institutional excellence as inseparable: a thriving institution is built from thriving people, and thriving people are those who grow with intention in a supportive environment.

Kaizen & Institutional Excellence in SrujanaSangama operates simultaneously at five levels:

| Level | Focus | Primary Users |
|---|---|---|
| **Individual** | Personal Productivity (knowledge wiki, GTD tasks, GPS steering), habits, wellbeing, performance reflection, Ikigai | Each faculty member |
| **Team** | Department or research group improvement cycles | HODs, research group leads |
| **Centre / School** | Programme quality, school-level culture and capability | Deans, Centre Directors |
| **University** | Institutional Development Plan (IDP), NIRF strategy, IQAC, accreditation | VC, Registrar, IQAC, leadership team |
| **Strategic** | Long-range vision, Chancellor's direction, school goal-setting, peer benchmarking | University leadership, REVA Summit |

### What Faculty and Leaders Do — and What the System Enables

**Individual: Personal Productivity**

Personal Productivity is the cross-domain layer through which every faculty member experiences SrujanaSangama day to day. It sits conceptually inside Kaizen & Institutional Excellence because disciplined, reflective self-management is itself a continuous improvement practice — but it operates across all five T.R.A.C.K. domains at once, aggregating commitments from teaching, research, admin, consulting, and personal life into one coherent picture. The system enables faculty to:

* Maintain a personal knowledge wiki — a structured, interlinked collection of markdown notes, built in the spirit of the LLM-wiki pattern: raw information (meeting notes, article highlights, conversation takeaways, ideas) is dropped into an inbox, and the system processes it into durable, cross-linked wiki pages organised by topic, course, project, or person
* Capture anything, anywhere, anytime into the inbox without needing to decide in the moment how or where it should be filed — the processing into structured wiki form happens afterward, as a deliberate step, not as a precondition for capture
* Browse and navigate the resulting wiki in a familiar linked-notes tool such as Obsidian, so that knowledge compounds across semesters and years rather than evaporating between sessions
* Run a complete Getting Things Done (GTD) task management cycle — capture, clarify, organise, reflect, and engage — so that nothing is held only in memory and every commitment has a clear next action
* Steer all tasks and projects through the GPS framework — **Goal** (the destination), **Plan** (the route), and **Sankalpa** (the resolution or committed intent that gives the goal its seriousness) — so that day-to-day task lists are never disconnected from why they matter
* Trace any single task upward through its Plan to its Goal to its Sankalpa, and downward from a Sankalpa to see every task currently serving it — giving faculty a constant, honest view of whether their daily effort matches their stated intentions

**Individual: GPS Goal Planning**

Purposeful professional life requires intentional goal architecture. The system enables faculty to:

* Set annual and semester Sankalpas — resolutions of serious personal intent — aligned to REVA's institutional commitments and personal Ikigai
* Translate each Sankalpa into one or more Goals, and each Goal into a concrete Plan with milestones, so that the chain from resolution to daily action is always visible and traceable
* Review goal alignment at semester boundaries and recalibrate based on progress, energy, and institutional context
* Map Goals explicitly across all five T.R.A.C.K. domains so that teaching, research, admin, consulting, and personal development are integrated rather than competing
* Receive AI-generated nudges, reflection prompts, and milestone reminders calibrated to their current GPS structure

**Individual: Weekly Review & Reflection**

Continuous improvement requires a disciplined review cycle. The system enables faculty to:

* Conduct a structured weekly review covering what went well, what didn't, what carries forward, and what to let go
* Surface cross-domain wins and blocks in a single integrated view — not siloed by domain
* Maintain a reflective log that builds personal memory and institutional wisdom across academic years
* Generate reflection summaries that can optionally feed into team-level or department-level improvement reviews

**Individual: Habit Design & Tracking**

Sustained performance is built on reliable habits. The system enables faculty to:

* Identify keystone habits and design implementation intentions aligned to current Goals
* Track daily and weekly commitments with streaks, recovery patterns, and energy signals
* Generate privacy-respecting progress reports for personal use and, on an opt-in basis, for institutional tracking
* Connect habit patterns to wellbeing indicators and professional performance trends

**Individual: Performance Management & Monthly Reporting**

Performance management at the individual level is about honest self-assessment and timely course correction — not just an annual ritual ahead of appraisal season. The system enables faculty and researchers to:

* Generate a monthly report that serves two purposes at once: a candid personal reflection on progress against GPS commitments, and a clean, shareable summary of accomplishments suitable to pass upward if the faculty member or institution wants visibility — with the two kept in clearly separated sections so reflection stays honest and the shareable portion stays presentable
* Track self-performance against role expectations — teaching load and quality, research output, administrative contribution, consulting engagement, and Kaizen participation — drawing on the same task and wiki data already being maintained, so nothing needs to be re-entered for reporting purposes
* Surface drift early: where current activity is diverging from stated Goals or Sankalpa, the system flags this in the monthly cycle rather than waiting for a semester or year to reveal the gap
* Prepare structured inputs for REVA's formal annual appraisal process — self-appraisal narrative, evidence compilation from the public-memory portfolio, and API-style score worksheets — so the formal appraisal becomes a compilation of already-tracked evidence rather than a last-minute reconstruction
* Use monthly reporting as a deliberate course-correction checkpoint: each month closes with an explicit decision to continue, adjust, or retire each active Goal

**Individual: Skill Development & FDP**

Faculty are learners first. The system enables them to:

* Identify skill gaps across teaching, research, administration, and technology domains
* Generate personal learning roadmaps with certification planning and learning log maintenance
* Select and schedule FDPs aligned to personal goals, course review feedback, and accreditation requirements
* Generate post-FDP reflection and practice integration plans: what will change, by when, and how it will be evidenced
* Track learning velocity and surface adjustment recommendations when progress stalls

**Individual: Wellbeing & Work-Life Integration**

Sustained faculty performance requires genuine care for the whole person. The system enables faculty to:

* Conduct wellbeing check-ins that surface stress signals, energy patterns, and boundary risks
* Design workload calibration and seasonal planning around exam periods, conference seasons, and accreditation cycles
* Access structured support navigation for mental health, physical health, and mindfulness practices — with confidentiality as a design principle, not an afterthought
* Engage with the Hitaishin (सु-हित) framework of wellbeing as a REVA institutional commitment
* Review Ikigai alignment periodically — mapping current work to purpose and adjusting direction where meaning has drifted

**Team & Department: Continuous Improvement Cycles**

Departments improve when individual reflection aggregates into collective intelligence. The system enables HODs and team leads to:

* Aggregate opt-in individual GPS signals into departmental improvement dashboards — identifying shared capability gaps, workload imbalances, and emerging strengths
* Design and track department-level improvement initiatives with milestones, owners, and progress documentation
* Facilitate peer learning sessions, Communities of Practice, and faculty learning circles with structured agendas and outcome records
* Coordinate FDP planning at the department level: identify collective gaps, plan cohort interventions, and track post-FDP behaviour change
* Generate department-level KPA/KRA alignment reports mapping faculty accomplishments to institutional improvement milestones
* Maintain a department improvement log that feeds into school-level and IQAC review cycles

**Centre / School: Programme Quality & Culture**

School-level Kaizen connects teaching quality, research culture, and student outcomes into a coherent improvement narrative. The system enables Deans and Centre Directors to:

* Maintain school-level quality dashboards covering CO attainment trends, research output, placement outcomes, accreditation readiness, and faculty development indicators
* Identify and act on early warning signals — declining CO attainment, placement gaps, research stagnation, faculty wellbeing risks — before they become crises
* Coordinate school-level best practice sharing: faculty who have solved a pedagogical problem, won a grant, or improved student outcomes share their approach in a structured, discoverable form
* Generate school-level inputs for IQAC, NIRF, and accreditation data cycles without manual data aggregation
* Coordinate intra-school innovation initiatives — curriculum redesign sprints, portfolio-first transitions, AI-readiness audits — with structured planning and outcome documentation
* Design and document a school improvement plan aligned to REVA's Vision, NIRF strategy, and accreditation calendar

**University: Institutional Kaizen & IDP**

Institutional Kaizen translates accumulated signals from individuals, departments, and schools into university-wide action, anchored by the **Institutional Development Plan (IDP)** — REVA's formal multi-year roadmap for achieving its Vision of becoming a technologically advanced, sustainable, global university. The system enables the VC office, Registrar, IQAC, and institutional leadership to:

* Maintain and track the Institutional Development Plan (IDP): articulating multi-year goals across T.R.A.C.K. domains, assigning ownership at school and department level, setting measurable milestones, and tracking progress quarterly — so the IDP is a living document rather than a filed report
* Maintain a live institutional health dashboard spanning all T.R.A.C.K. domains — teaching quality, research momentum, administrative efficiency, commercial engagement, and community wellbeing
* Aggregate anonymous, opt-in individual and team Kaizen data into institutional intelligence: where are the systemic strengths, where are the persistent gaps, what is the direction of travel
* Run and document the Academic and Administrative Audit (AAA) cycle with structured observation reports, corrective action tracking, and evidence of change
* Operate a university-wide suggestion and idea incubator: any faculty member or administrator can surface an improvement idea; the system routes it to the relevant authority with structured follow-through and closure
* Track action taken reports (ATRs) on all accreditation recommendations, regulatory observations, and IDP commitments — with owners, deadlines, and evidence
* Generate the Annual Quality Assurance Report (AQAR) from live institutional data — not manual compilation — with narrative, data, and evidence in NAAC-required format
* Monitor and improve faculty wellbeing at the institutional level, identifying workload concentration, recognition gaps, and support needs as inputs to HR policy and leadership attention
* Align IDP milestones to the REVA Summit cycle: each Summit reviews IDP progress, resets school-level targets, and refreshes institutional priorities in light of the Chancellor's vision and benchmarking findings

**Strategic: REVA Manthan & REVA Summit**

Strategic Kaizen at REVA is institutionalised through two deliberative processes. **REVA Manthan** is the operational strategic review — held periodically — where leadership, faculty leaders, and key stakeholders assess T.R.A.C.K. performance, identify improvement gaps, and commit to priorities for the coming cycle. **REVA Summit** is the annual strategic meeting convened under the Chancellor's vision — where schools and centres set their goals for the year, benchmark against peer institutions, and align their plans to REVA's long-range institutional direction.

The system supports both processes by enabling leadership to:

* Compile comprehensive institutional performance briefs for Manthan and the REVA Summit: T.R.A.C.K. domain performance, NIRF trajectory, accreditation status, research output trends, placement quality, financial sustainability, and community wellbeing — assembled from live data rather than manual collection
* Generate school-wise and centre-wise performance summaries so that each unit arrives at the REVA Summit with a clear picture of its own standing and gaps
* Generate benchmarking analyses comparing REVA schools and centres against equivalent departments and schools in peer institutions — nationally and internationally — as structured inputs for Summit goal-setting discussions
* Translate the Chancellor's vision and direction into structured goal-setting frameworks for each school and centre, so Summit deliberations produce concrete, measurable commitments rather than aspirational statements
* Facilitate structured strategic dialogue by providing discussion frameworks, evidence packs, scenario analyses, and decision templates — so deliberation is informed and time-efficient
* Document Manthan and Summit deliberations: decisions made, priorities committed, owners identified, and milestones defined — feeding directly into the next IDP cycle
* Track strategic plan execution across all T.R.A.C.K. domains and report progress quarterly to leadership — surfacing slippage early and enabling timely intervention
* Connect Summit commitments back to individual and department GPS goals, so that the university's direction is visible and meaningful at every level of the institution
* Support REVA's long-range Vision planning — Top 100 NIRF, globally recognised, dedicated to the wellbeing of all — with structured horizon-scanning, scenario planning, and milestone architecture for leadership review and refinement

## Design Principles

Every capability in SrujanaSangama is grounded in the following non-negotiable principles:

* **Human-AI collaboration at every step** — AI accelerates, drafts, analyses, and surfaces options; humans exercise judgment, build relationships, and take final decisions. No curriculum, assessment, governance action, or strategic decision proceeds on AI output alone.
* **REVA Vision as the compass** — Technologically advanced, sustainable, global, dedicated to the wellbeing of all. Every design choice is tested against this.
* **REVA Mission as the design brief** — Learner-centred, entrepreneurial stewardship, liberal arts and creative disciplines, collaboration, universal values, and sustainability. These are structural requirements, not decorative statements.
* **ADDIE as the operating backbone** — Analysis always precedes Design; Development is validated before Implementation; Evaluation feeds back into every phase.
* **NBA OBE as the quality contract** — Every course artefact is traceable to a CO; every CO is mapped to a PO; attainment is computed, evidenced, and audited.
* **PEOs raised for the AI era** — Graduates are expected to perform at 5–6 year experience levels through AI co-working; COs embed this; assessments test judgment, synthesis, creation, and ethics.
* **Portfolio-first learning** — Every course produces a tangible artefact: a publication, product, open source contribution, or documented solution.
* **Enterprising human skills as the durable advantage** — Critical thinking, communication, ethical reasoning, adaptability, collaboration, initiative, and creativity are non-negotiable across every programme. These are what AI cannot replace.
* **Srujana as the student's personal narrative** — Every student has a visible journey from skill formation to real-world contribution. SrujanaSangama scaffolds and celebrates this journey in partnership with SrujanaBuddy.
* **Differentiation by design** — Fast learners are accelerated through honours tracks and early Srujana Stage 3. Slow learners are supported through AI tutors, extra tutorials, and personalised pathways. No student is without a forward path.
* **Bloom's taxonomy as the learning compass** — Outcomes, content, activities, and assessments are coherently aligned; HOTS dominates in AI-era courses.
* **AI-readiness of assessments** — Trivially AI-completable tasks are redesigned. Proctored activities verify genuine competency. AI-assisted work is explicit, bounded, and declared.
* **Futures intelligence as a living input** — Curriculum is updated by WEF, NASSCOM, LinkedIn, and global signals — synthesised by AI, deliberated by humans, approved by BOS.
* **Governance integrity** — Industry validation, BOS approval, Academic Council deliberation, and Board of Management decisions are all fundamentally human processes. AI prepares, structures, and documents; humans decide.
* **Administrative intelligence across all ten verticals** — Every operational function from Admissions to IQAC is a candidate for AI augmentation: reducing manual effort, improving data quality, surfacing insights earlier, and ensuring nothing falls through the cracks.
* **Internationalisation as a long-term human relationship** — Twinning, dual degrees, and joint programmes are built on trust, sustained dialogue, and mutual academic respect — supported by AI intelligence, driven by human partnership.
* **Kaizen & Institutional Excellence at all levels** — Personal Productivity (knowledge wiki, GTD tasks, GPS steering), habits, department improvement cycles, school quality planning, the Institutional Development Plan (IDP), and strategic transformation through REVA Manthan and REVA Summit — all are continuous, not periodic.
* **GPS as the thread from intention to action** — Every task traces to a Plan, every Plan to a Goal, every Goal to a Sankalpa. No daily action should be untraceable to why it matters.
* **Strategy grounded in evidence, not instinct alone** — REVA Manthan and REVA Summit deliberations are informed by live institutional data, school-wise benchmarking analyses, and structured goal-setting frameworks prepared by the platform — so strategic conversations are richer, school targets are evidence-based, and decisions are better.
* **NIRF Top 100 as the institutional north star** — Every platform capability connects to research output, academic quality, placements, faculty excellence, and global engagement.
* **REVA brand on every output** — All documents, pages, and slides conform to REVA visual identity, typography, and brand voice.
