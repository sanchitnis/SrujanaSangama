# India Research Context

## Institutional Identity — REVA University

REVA University is a private deemed-to-be university in Bengaluru, Karnataka, with schools spanning Engineering & Technology, Sciences, Management, Law, Humanities, Architecture, and Commerce. Faculty research at REVA operates within the following strategic context:

- **NIRF Ranking**: Research output, citation impact, and PhD completions are direct NIRF inputs (Research & Professional Practice parameter)
- **NAAC Accreditation**: Criterion III (Research, Innovations & Extension) is directly improved by externally funded projects and publications
- **NBA**: Programme-level accreditation requires faculty publications to demonstrate research-active culture
- **NEP 2020**: Mandates multidisciplinary research, faculty development, and integration of Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS)
- **Institutional Strengths**: REVA has emerging strengths in AI/ML, sustainable infrastructure, legal tech, health sciences, and social innovation

## NEP 2020 Research Mandates for Faculty

Faculty research at REVA should align with NEP 2020 priorities:

1. **Multidisciplinary research** — proposals crossing two or more school boundaries are viewed favourably
2. **Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS)** — integrating traditional knowledge with modern research is a specific NEP thrust
3. **Community-connected research** — locally relevant problems with demonstrable societal impact
4. **Industry linkage** — research with a clear path to technology transfer or startup formation
5. **International collaboration** — joint publications and student exchange as research outputs

## NIRF Research & Professional Practice (RP) Parameter

The RP parameter contributes 30 points to NIRF ranking. Sub-parameters faculty research directly impacts:

| Sub-parameter | Weight | Driver |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| Combined Metric for Publications (CMP) | 30% | Scopus/WoS publications, citations, h-index |
| Combined Metric for Patents (CMP-P) | 30% | Patents filed and granted |
| Funded Projects | 20% | External funding amount and project count |
| PhDs Awarded | 20% | Annual PhD completions |

**Strategic implication**: A single Scopus Q1 paper with 10+ citations contributes more to NIRF than 5 conference papers. Faculty should be advised to target quality over quantity.

## Karnataka & Regional Context

- **Karnataka State S&T Council (KSCST)**: State-level grants for technology development projects. Lower competition than national agencies. Good first grant for junior faculty.
- **VGST (Vision Group on Science & Technology)**: Karnataka government grants for young scientists and interdisciplinary research.
- **BIRAC (Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council)**: Bengaluru-based; excellent for health tech, medtech startups emerging from faculty research.
- **Bengaluru Tech Ecosystem**: Proximity to MNCs (Infosys, Wipro, NASSCOM, TCS) creates unique opportunities for industry-sponsored research and CSR-funded projects.
