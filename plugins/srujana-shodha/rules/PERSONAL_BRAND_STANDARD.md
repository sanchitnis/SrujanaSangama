# Personal Brand Standard

## Why Personal Brand Matters for Faculty

Academic impact is not just about publishing — it is about being *found, cited, and invited*. A faculty member with a strong personal brand:

- Gets cited more (discoverability → citations → NIRF RP score)
- Attracts collaboration requests
- Gets invited to review, keynote, and advise
- Opens industry consulting and CSR funding conversations
- Advances their career faster (promotions, awards, recognition)

---

## The Six Pillars of Academic Personal Brand

### Pillar 1: Verified Digital Presence

Every faculty member must have these four profiles **active and consistent**:

| Platform | Purpose | Priority |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **ORCID** (orcid.org) | Unique researcher identifier — links all publications regardless of name variations | 🔴 Critical |
| **Google Scholar** | Public publication list, citations, h-index, i10-index | 🔴 Critical |
| **ResearchGate** | Open access sharing, collaboration discovery, RG Score | 🟡 Important |
| **LinkedIn** | Industry visibility, CSR/consulting connections, thought leadership posts | 🟡 Important |
| **Scopus Author Profile** | Auto-generated but must be claimed, verified, and merged if duplicated | 🟡 Important |

**Quality criteria for each profile:**
- Photo: Professional, not a selfie or cropped group photo
- Bio: 150-word third-person summary including expertise, key publications, and current research focus
- All significant publications listed and correctly attributed
- At least one contact method visible

---

### Pillar 2: Publication Discoverability

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Upload preprints to arXiv, SSRN, or ResearchGate | Increases citation by 30–100% (open access effect) |
| Use keywords strategically in title/abstract | Improves indexing in Scopus/WoS |
| Write a plain-language abstract version (1 paragraph) | For ResearchGate, LinkedIn, and media pitches |
| Add ORCID to every submission | Links publication to author permanently |

---

### Pillar 3: Conference Pathway

A strategic conference presence builds reputation faster than publications alone:

**Year 1 (Emerging)**: 2 paper presentations at national Scopus-indexed conferences
**Year 2 (Active)**: 1 international conference + 1 invited talk at another institution
**Year 3 (Recognised)**: Session chair or organising committee role; 1 workshop conducted
**Year 4+ (Established)**: Keynote speaker; editorial board membership; symposium organisation

**Conference selection criteria**: Is it Scopus-indexed? Is the audience your target collaborator community? Does it publish in a special issue (double benefit)?

---

### Pillar 4: LinkedIn Thought Leadership

Faculty who post 1–2 LinkedIn updates per week from their research domain build audiences faster than those who post only when papers are accepted.

**Content formula (rotate weekly):**
1. **Research insight** — share one finding from your work in plain language
2. **Field trend** — comment on a recent development in your discipline
3. **Behind the scenes** — what you're currently working on, challenges you're facing
4. **Student spotlight** — highlight a student project or achievement (with permission)

**Frequency**: 1 post/week minimum; 2–3 posts/week for rapid audience building.

---

### Pillar 5: Media & Press Visibility

Research with societal impact deserves media attention. A press release from REVA's communications team amplifies reach.

**Media-ready criteria for a research finding:**
- Solves a local or national problem people can relate to
- Has a concrete result or prototype (not just theoretical)
- Can be explained in 2 sentences to a non-expert

**Outreach channels in India:**
- The Hindu (Science & Technology section)
- Deccan Herald (Bengaluru — local reach)
- Down to Earth (environment/sustainability)
- LiveMint (tech and policy)
- PIB (Press Information Bureau — for government-aligned research)

---

### Pillar 6: Awards & Recognition Pathway

**Awards to target by career stage:**

| Career Stage | Awards/Recognition |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Early career (0–5 yrs post-PhD) | DST INSPIRE Faculty Award, SERB Young Scientist Award, Karnataka Rajyotsava Award (science category) |
| Mid career (5–10 yrs) | ISCA Young Scientist Award, VGST Young Scientist Award, institutional research excellence awards |
| Established (10+ yrs) | Fellowship of learned societies (FNAE, FIE, FASc), National Science Academy membership, international fellow status |

## Brand Audit Checklist

Run this checklist at the start of the brand-sprint workflow:

- [ ] ORCID created and verified
- [ ] Google Scholar profile claimed and all publications verified
- [ ] ResearchGate profile complete with at least 3 full-text uploads
- [ ] LinkedIn profile has researcher bio, publications listed, and profile photo
- [ ] Scopus Author ID claimed (check for duplicates)
- [ ] Email signature includes ORCID link
- [ ] Institution website profile page is current
- [ ] At least 1 media-ready research brief prepared
