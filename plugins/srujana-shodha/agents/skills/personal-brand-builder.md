## Platform Setup & Optimisation Guide

### 1. ORCID (orcid.org) — CRITICAL FIRST STEP

**Setup checklist:**
```
□ Create account at orcid.org (free, 5 minutes)
□ Add full legal name + preferred name as it appears on publications
□ Set profile visibility to "Everyone" (not "Trusted parties")
□ Add education history (PhD institution, year)
□ Add employment (REVA University, current role, start date)
□ Import works from Scopus or Crossref (auto-populate publications)
□ Add the ORCID iD to: email signature, CV, all future manuscript submissions
□ Link ORCID to Scopus Author Profile
```

**Bio template for ORCID:**
```
[Name] is [Role] in the Department of [X] at REVA University, Bengaluru. 
Research expertise: [Zone A 1], [Zone A 2], and [Zone B 1]. 
Current research: [active project in one sentence]. 
Contact: [institutional email].
```

### 2. Google Scholar — HIGH-VISIBILITY PLATFORM

**Setup and maintenance:**
```
□ Create profile at scholar.google.com
□ Upload professional photo (same as LinkedIn)
□ Add all affiliations (current + PhD institution)
□ Set co-author email alerts
□ Verify all publications are correctly attributed to you
□ Merge duplicate papers
□ Set up Google Alerts for your name to catch citations
□ Share the profile link in email signature and social media bio
```

**Key metrics to track monthly:**
- Total citations
- h-index
- i10-index
- Most-cited papers (these are your brand anchors)

### 3. ResearchGate — OPEN ACCESS & COLLABORATION

```
□ Create account with institutional email
□ Upload full-text PDFs for all papers (check copyright permissions first)
□ Use "Request full-text" option for papers you don't own
□ Complete the Research Interest section with 5–8 specific topics
□ Follow researchers in your field
□ Respond to questions about your research (builds RG Score)
□ Share preprints before journal acceptance to establish priority
```

### 4. LinkedIn — INDUSTRY & THOUGHT LEADERSHIP

**Profile optimisation:**
```
□ Headline: "[Role] | [Research Domain] | REVA University"
   Example: "Associate Professor | AI for Healthcare | REVA University"
□ About section: 150-word researcher bio (third person or first person — choose one)
□ Featured section: pin top 3 publications (link to Scholar/RG)
□ Add all publications under Publications section
□ Skills: add 5 research-relevant skills; ask colleagues to endorse
□ Enable "Creator Mode" to unlock newsletter and follower analytics
□ Set custom URL: linkedin.com/in/[firstname-lastname]
```

**Content strategy (rotate these 4 types weekly):**

| Week | Post Type | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Week 1 | Research Insight | "One finding from my latest paper that surprised us..." |
| Week 2 | Field Trend | "Here's what's changing in [domain] in 2025..." |
| Week 3 | Behind the Scenes | "Currently designing the methodology for my new project on..." |
| Week 4 | Student/Community | "Proud of this project by my research student [name]..." |

**Posting rules:**
- 3–5 hashtags per post (field-specific)
- Always include a visual (paper screenshot, infographic, photo)
- Engage with comments within 24 hours (algorithm rewards this)
- Never post more than once a day

### 5. Scopus Author Profile — CLAIM & CLEAN

```
□ Go to scopus.com/freelookup/form/author
□ Search your name and find all papers
□ Create a Scopus account and claim your profile
□ Request merge if multiple author IDs exist for you
□ Verify affiliation is current (REVA University)
□ Link Scopus ID to ORCID
```

## The 90-Day Brand Sprint

A structured plan for faculty starting from scratch or reviving a dormant brand.

### Month 1 — Foundation (Platform Week by Week)

| Week | Priority Task | Time Needed |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| Week 1 | Create/update ORCID + Google Scholar | 3–4 hours |
| Week 2 | Set up/update ResearchGate + upload 3 papers | 2–3 hours |
| Week 3 | Optimise LinkedIn profile fully | 3–4 hours |
| Week 4 | Claim Scopus Author ID + merge duplicates | 1–2 hours |

**Month 1 milestone**: All 5 platforms active and consistent.

### Month 2 — Content & Visibility

| Week | Content Task | Platform |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Week 5 | Write plain-language summary of top paper | LinkedIn + ResearchGate |
| Week 6 | Post a "work in progress" update on current research | LinkedIn |
| Week 7 | Comment meaningfully on 5 posts by researchers in your field | LinkedIn |
| Week 8 | Submit abstract to 1 conference in your domain | Conference portal |

**Month 2 milestone**: 4 pieces of content published; 1 conference abstract submitted.

### Month 3 — Recognition & Reach

| Week | Action | Platform |
|------|--------|---------|
| Week 9 | Draft 1-page media brief on your most impactful finding | Email to REVA Communications |
| Week 10 | Identify 1 award to apply for (see PERSONAL_BRAND_STANDARD.md) | Award portal |
| Week 11 | Write to 1 journal editor offering to review (builds peer recognition) | Email |
| Week 12 | Review and update all profiles; set next-quarter targets | All platforms |

**Month 3 milestone**: 8 pieces of content; 1 media outreach; 1 award identified; review offer sent.

## Key Behaviours

- **Quick wins first**: ORCID + Google Scholar in one session — these have the highest leverage per hour invested
- **Consistency across platforms**: same photo, same bio, same name format everywhere
- **Quality over frequency**: 1 thoughtful LinkedIn post per week beats 5 generic ones
- **Never fabricate metrics**: if citation count is 0, it's 0. The brand grows from honest effort.
- **Track progress**: update `memory/semantic/brand-profile.md` after every brand session
