# Session Type: Thesis Sprint & Pre-Submission Preparation

**Purpose:** Plan and execute thesis chapter writing, enforce Appendix 4 format compliance, coach the pre-submission colloquium, and run the final submission checklist.

**Estimated total time:** 45–90 minutes depending on chapter stage

**Reference files:** `references/thesis-format-guide.md` (Appendix 4 rules), `references/reva-phd-regulations-digest.md` (§14.2, §14.3, §14.4)

---

## Phase 1 — Thesis Progress Read (5 min)

Read `context/research-tracker.md` → `thesis_chapters_complete` field.

Display current chapter status:
```
| Chapter | Status | Last updated |
|---|---|---|
| Prelims (title, cert, ToC, abstract) | [done/in-progress/not-started] | [date] |
| Ch 1 — Introduction | ... | ... |
| Ch 2 — Literature Review | ... | ... |
| Ch 3 — Methodology | ... | ... |
| Ch 4–6+ — Research Work | ... | ... |
| Final Chapter — Conclusion | ... | ... |
| References + Appendices + Evidence | ... | ... |
```

Ask: *"Which chapter or section are we working on today?"*

---

## Phase 2 — Chapter Writing Sprint (30–45 min)

Activate `thesis-writer.md`.

For the selected chapter:
1. Confirm the chapter's primary argument or purpose
2. Review the scholar's existing draft (if any)
3. Identify gaps vs. the chapter structure from `thesis-writer.md`
4. Help write or refine specific sections

**Format check at every draft review:**
- Font ≥ 11pt? Body spacing 1.5× or double?
- Margins correct (1.5 inch binding, 1 inch others)?
- Pagination: Roman for prelims, continuous Arabic for body?
- No REVA logo, no dedication page, no personal address?
- Cross-reference `references/thesis-format-guide.md` for full checklist

**Plagiarism check (§14.4b-ii):** If the chapter seems heavily paraphrased, flag for Turnitin/iThenticate check. Target: below 8% per chapter to stay safely under the 10% overall ceiling.

**Language discipline (CONSTITUTION §15):** Flag any use of "significant" without a stated p-value.

---

## Phase 3 — Pre-Submission Colloquium Preparation (15–20 min)

Applicable when the scholar is within 4–8 weeks of the colloquium.

Review the §14.2 6-step mandatory process:
1. Letter of intent — has this been submitted?
2. Internal review (DAC/DRPC) — date and feedback received?
3. Corrections list — received and implemented?
4. Colloquium scheduled?
5. If previously failed: re-appearance ≥1 month gap confirmed?

Colloquium presentation coaching (20–25 min presentation):
- Problem statement and evolved objectives (vs. synopsis)
- Methodology and validity discussion
- Results and contributions
- Limitations and future work
- Mock Q&A with standard doctoral committee questions

---

## Phase 4 — Final Submission Checklist (10 min)

When the scholar believes the thesis is ready, run the Appendix 4 checklist:

- [ ] 5 bound hard copies prepared
- [ ] CD/DVD with PDF of full thesis
- [ ] 1 journal paper reprint or acceptance letter (main contribution paper)
- [ ] 2 conference certificates (with guide as co-author)
- [ ] INFLIBNET deposit confirmation (§16)
- [ ] Plagiarism report (Turnitin / iThenticate) — within 10%
- [ ] DAC/DRPC approval signature on Certificate page
- [ ] Submission by: Batch 2014–2019: Nov 30 / Batch 2020+: Apr 30 (§14.4)
- [ ] Within 6-month fee-exemption window from synopsis submission? (§14.4)

Flag any unchecked item.

---

## Phase 5 — Examiner Panel Reminder (2 min)

Remind the scholar that the synopsis submission triggers the §14.3 examiner panel process:
- Panel of 12 (6 Karnataka, 6 outside incl. ≥1 abroad), h-index ≥5 recommended
- BoS Chair assigned within 4 weeks of synopsis submission
- REVA selects 2 examiners from the panel
