# Session Type: Entrance Preparation & Guide Eligibility

**Purpose:** Help a prospective PhD scholar prepare for the entrance examination and guide identification process before provisional registration.

**Estimated total time:** 45–60 minutes

---

## Phase 1 — Topic Ideation (15 min)

*"Before thinking about the exam, let's think about what you actually want to research. That clarity will sharpen your interview performance."*

Activate `topic-scout.md`:
- Research area mapping (4 questions)
- 3–5 topic shortlist with feasibility assessment
- Ikigai check for each top candidate

Outcome: Scholar has a preferred research area with clear gap statement.

---

## Phase 2 — Guide Identification (10 min)

Apply §7.2 guide eligibility check:

1. *"Do you have a faculty member in mind as a guide? If yes, I can help you assess fit."*
2. If yes: check their research area alignment, current scholar load (cap: 8 under §7.2), and department
3. If no: help the scholar identify 2–3 candidate guides based on their research topic shortlist

Discuss co-guide options for interdisciplinary topics (§3.13, §7.1d).

Outcome: 1–2 guide candidates with a clear approach plan.

---

## Phase 3 — Entrance Test Preparation (15 min)

The REVA PhD entrance test typically covers:
- Research Methodology (RM) — key concepts: research design, hypothesis formulation, literature review, sampling, validity
- Subject-specific component — CSE/CSA fundamentals relevant to the prospective research area

### RM Quick Review (5 questions)
Ask the scholar:
1. *"What is the difference between a research question and a hypothesis?"*
2. *"Name two types of research validity and explain what each means."*
3. *"What is a systematic literature review, and how does it differ from a general literature survey?"*
4. *"What is triangulation in research methodology?"*
5. *"What does 'replication' mean in empirical research?"*

After each answer: correct gently, reinforce key terminology.

### Subject-Specific Prep
Ask: *"Which area of CSE is your entrance test likely to focus on for your proposed topic?"*

Provide a topic-specific quick review (key concepts, definitions, current state of the art, 5–10 important papers in the area).

---

## Phase 4 — Interview Coaching (15 min)

The guide meeting / pre-registration interview typically covers:

| Question | What the Committee Is Assessing |
|---|---|
| *"Why this research area?"* | Genuine motivation vs. opportunistic interest |
| *"What is the gap in current literature?"* | Literature awareness |
| *"What methodology will you use?"* | Research thinking |
| *"What do you expect to publish?"* | Ambition and realism |
| *"Why REVA?"* | Commitment |

Mock Q&A: ask the scholar each question → coach the answer → repeat until confident.

---

## Phase 5 — 10-Minute Presentation Dry Run (5 min)

Prospective scholars are sometimes asked to present a research proposal in 10 minutes. Structure:
1. Problem statement (2 min)
2. Literature gap (2 min)
3. Proposed research approach (3 min)
4. Expected contributions (2 min)
5. Timeline (1 min)

Ask the scholar to sketch each slide's talking points. Identify weak sections.
