# Partner-Type Checklist Variations

Load the relevant section for the partner type identified in Step 0.

---

## A. Indian University Partner

**Additional checks beyond standard review:**

- [ ] Is the Indian university UGC-recognised? Verify on UGC website (ugc.gov.in)
- [ ] If joint/dual degree: UGC prior approval obtained or planned?
- [ ] If twinning program: UGC sanction letter attached or referenced?
- [ ] Credit transfer aligned with UGC Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) norms?
- [ ] Student fee treatment for exchange students clarified (Fee Regulatory Committee implications)?
- [ ] Faculty deputation: Home university salary continuation confirmed?
- [ ] Research collaboration: Which institution's Ethics Committee (IEC/IAEC) governs?
- [ ] No exclusivity that prevents REVA from partnering with other Indian universities in the same city/discipline?

**Common attempt by counterparty to watch for:**
- Implying REVA is the "junior" institution by assigning all administrative burden to REVA
- Vague deliverables that ultimately require REVA to host all activities

## B. Foreign University Partner

**Additional checks beyond standard review:**

- [ ] UGC approval required? (Joint degree / twinning / franchise arrangement → YES)
- [ ] FCRA analysis: Any financial flow from foreign university? If yes, FCRA registration/prior permission required
- [ ] FEMA compliance: Any outward/inward remittances?
- [ ] Governing law must be Indian law; foreign law is RED
- [ ] Dispute resolution seat must be in India; foreign seat is RED
- [ ] Data transfer: DPDPA 2023 cross-border transfer compliance for student/faculty data
- [ ] If EU partner: GDPR compliance and SCCs if data flows to EU
- [ ] Academic credential recognition: Degrees awarded by collaboration recognised in India by AIU / equivalency body?
- [ ] Visa/immigration: MOU should not commit REVA to visa sponsorship (beyond recommendation letters)
- [ ] Language of instruction and examination for exchange students specified?
- [ ] FCRA-exempt pure academic exchange vs. funded collaboration — get legal opinion if ambiguous

**Common attempt by counterparty to watch for:**
- Foreign governing law and arbitration seat in their jurisdiction
- IP ownership of jointly developed research assigned to foreign university as "lead"
- REVA obligated to pay tuition waiver for inbound students with no reciprocal waiver

## C. Industry Partner (Indian Company)

**Additional checks beyond standard review:**

- [ ] Is the company a legitimate entity? Check MCA21 registration (company/LLP)
- [ ] Purpose: Internship / Placement / Sponsored Research / Consultancy / Curriculum co-design — each has different obligations
- [ ] Sponsored research: Does the company's IP clause try to capture all outputs? (RED if yes)
- [ ] Internship/placement: MOU should not constitute a binding placement guarantee (academic autonomy risk)
- [ ] CSR funding: If company funds REVA under CSR (Companies Act 2013, s.135), verify CSR eligibility of activity
- [ ] GST: If REVA renders taxable services (lab access, testing, consultancy), GST responsibility must be specified
- [ ] Student data: Any sharing of student resume/data with company requires DPDPA 2023 consent mechanism
- [ ] Non-compete: Any clause restricting REVA from partnering with competitor companies is RED
- [ ] Curriculum co-design: REVA retains academic authority; industry inputs are advisory, not binding
- [ ] Branding: Any clause requiring REVA to display company branding in classrooms/events should be reviewed for REVA's brand policy compliance

**Common attempt by counterparty to watch for:**
- IP assignment clause capturing all student projects, capstone work, or thesis produced using company data/tools
- "Exclusive academic partner" language restricting REVA from working with competitors
- Company trying to make REVA liable for student performance during internship
- Proprietary tool/platform NDA that students must sign before accessing — check if this conflicts with REVA's academic policies

## D. Foreign Industry Partner (MNC / Foreign Company)

All checks from C (Industry Partner) PLUS:

- [ ] FCRA: If company provides grants, equipment, or monetary benefits → FCRA analysis required
- [ ] FEMA: Cross-border payments must be through authorised dealer
- [ ] Governing law: Indian law required; foreign law is RED
- [ ] Export control: If collaboration involves dual-use technology, research data, or defence applications → SCOMET/export control compliance check
- [ ] Anti-bribery: UK Bribery Act / FCPA exposure for foreign company — include mutual anti-corruption clause
- [ ] Data localisation: If company processes Indian student data on foreign servers — DPDPA 2023 cross-border transfer compliance

**Common attempt by counterparty to watch for:**
- Entire agreement governed by law of company's home country
- Mandatory arbitration in foreign jurisdiction (London, Singapore, New York) — expensive and impractical for REVA
- Broad IP assignment covering anything touching the company's products/platforms

## E. Government Body (Central / State / PSU)

**Additional checks beyond standard review:**

- [ ] Funding: Is this a government grant? If Central Government: Grant-in-Aid rules apply; check utilisation certificate requirements
- [ ] MoU with Ministry / Department: May require Cabinet approval or Secretary-level sign-off on their side — verify
- [ ] PSU collaboration: PSU's board approval may be required for commitments above threshold
- [ ] RTI: Government bodies are subject to RTI; information shared with them may become disclosable — flag to REVA legal counsel
- [ ] Audit: Government-funded activities are subject to CAG/statutory audit; REVA must maintain accounts accordingly
- [ ] Intellectual property: Central government retains march-in rights for government-funded research (check grant terms)
- [ ] No clause making REVA an "implementing agency" with attendant legal liability without adequate indemnification

---

## F. NGO / Civil Society / International Organisation

**Additional checks beyond standard review:**

- [ ] NGO legitimacy: Is the NGO registered (Societies Registration Act / Trust Act / Section 8 Company)? Check registration
- [ ] Foreign NGO: FCRA registration of foreign NGO required for receiving funds in India; REVA receiving funds from foreign NGO → REVA's FCRA status applies
- [ ] International Organisation (UN agency, World Bank, etc.): These have privileges and immunities — check if dispute resolution clause needs to accommodate
- [ ] CSR channel: If NGO is channelling company CSR funds to REVA — verify CSR policy compliance
- [ ] Reputational check: Is the NGO's mission and public reputation consistent with REVA's values and brand?
- [ ] Scope creep: NGO MOUs often expand in scope informally; ensure scope clause is precise and amendment requires written agreement
