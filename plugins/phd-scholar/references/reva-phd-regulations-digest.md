# REVA PhD Regulations — Quick Reference Digest

Source: `references/reva-PhD-regulations.md` (provided 2026-06-01). All rules cite their section number. This is a working digest — always consult the source document for the full text.

## 2. Credit Requirements (§9.7)

| Candidate Type | Minimum Credits |
|---|---|
| Full-time / Part-time (standard) | 18 |
| Industrial / Experienced professional | 30 |
| 4-year UG degree holder (B.Tech/BE) | 46 |
| Foreign scholar or other-domain entry | 50 |

Credits must be earned before the Research Proposal Evaluation (RPE). Credits include coursework (Internal Assessment + CWEE), self-study, and ARM modules.

## 3. Publication Minimums — 2018+ Batch (§14.1)

Scholar must meet ONE of the following three options before thesis submission. Scholar must be the main/first author; guide/co-guide must be co-authors.

### Option A
- 3 papers in Scopus / WoS / UGC-approved peer-reviewed journals
- + 1 paper in a reputed conference (with ISBN/ISSN, evidence at thesis end)

### Option B
- 2 papers in Scopus / WoS / UGC-approved peer-reviewed journals
- + 1 granted patent (filed and granted; not just applied)
- + 1 paper in a reputed conference

### Option C
- 2 papers in Q1 or Q2 journals
- + 1 paper in a reputed conference

**Engineering / Applied Sciences floor:** Regardless of option, at least 1 paper must be in a Q1, Q2, or Q3 journal. (§14.1)

**At least 1 paper must be in active status (submitted/under-review) at the time of thesis submission.** (§14.1)

## 4. Pre-Submission Colloquium (§14.2)

Mandatory for all scholars. The colloquium is an oral evaluation of thesis readiness.

**6-step process:**
1. Scholar submits a Letter of Intent to the guide
2. Guide certifies readiness to the DRPC Chair
3. DRPC Chair schedules colloquium with internal panel
4. Colloquium conducted — scholar presents research; panel evaluates
5. If passed: guide signs off on thesis submission permission
6. If failed: re-appearance permitted after a minimum gap of **1 month**

The colloquium must be completed before thesis submission. Change of thesis title (§18c) is permitted only before the pre-submission colloquium.

## 6. Thesis Submission Deadlines (§14.4)

| Batch | Submission Deadline |
|---|---|
| 2014–2019 | 30 November of the eligible year |
| 2020 and beyond | 30 April of the eligible year |

**"Eligible year"** = first year when minimum duration has elapsed and all publication requirements are met.

**6-month fee exemption window:** No additional fee is charged if the scholar submits within 6 months of the guide's certification letter. (§14.4)

---

## 7. Plagiarism Ceiling (§14.4b-ii)

- Overall similarity ceiling: **10%**
- Excluded from check: direct quotations, bibliography/references, table of contents, scholar's own previously published papers, standard symbols and units
- Plagiarism report must be attached to the thesis at submission

---

## 8. Viva Voce (§15.4)

- Open viva voce — conducted in the presence of other scholars, faculty, and guests
- At least 1 external examiner must be physically present or attend by video conferencing (VC is explicitly permitted)
- The Doctoral Committee and the guide must be present
- A mock viva is permitted and recommended prior to the formal viva

---

## 9. INFLIBNET Deposit (§16)

The approved thesis must be submitted to INFLIBNET/Shodhganga BEFORE the award is announced. This is a mandatory precondition for the PhD degree to be conferred.

---

## 10. Change Rules (§18)

| Change | Permitted? | Condition | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Change of thesis title | Yes | Only before the pre-submission colloquium | §18c |
| Change of guide | Yes, but exceptional | Only in circumstances approved by DRPC; requires Registrar sign-off | §18d |
| Change of co-guide | Yes | As approved by guide and DRPC | §18d |

---

## 11. Guide Supervision Cap (§7.2)

A faculty member may supervise a maximum of **8 PhD scholars** at any point in time (including internal and external scholars).
