# Session Type: Prior Art Search & Novelty Modification

## Purpose
Search the Google Patents database using extraction-based search queries, analyze overlaps, and guide the user through brainstorming pivots to appropriately modify their idea for maximum patentability and novelty under IPO guidelines.

## Steps

### Phase 1: Keyword Extraction & Query Construction [10 min]
- Extract technical keywords, application area, and structural elements from the user's invention details.
- Formulate Google Patents search queries (e.g. `site:patents.google.com "technical keywords" "application area"`).

### Phase 2: Google Patents Database Querying [15 min]
- Execute search queries against Google Patents to identify the closest prior art documents.
- Retrieve and list the top relevant patents found with their patent numbers, titles, and publication years.

### Phase 3: Prior Art Analysis & Overlap Mapping [15 min]
- Analyze the found prior art against the user's proposed invention.
- Highlight specific features or claims that overlap, flagging any potential novelty or inventive step conflicts.

### Phase 4: Idea Modification & Novelty Brainstorming [20 min]
- Present the overlapping prior art details to the user.
- Guide the user through specific modification recommendations:
  - Restricting or broadening claim scopes
  - Incorporating unique architectural or procedural steps
  - Aligning with Section 3 exclusions bypass strategies (e.g. software/hardware integration)
- Document the finalized design changes to the invention.

## Output Template

### 📊 Prior Art Search Dashboard
- **Search Queries Used**: [List of queries]
- **Relevant Patents Identified**:
  - [Patent ID] - [Title] ([Year])

### 🔍 Overlap Assessment
- **Overlapping Features**: [Detailed explanation of matching/similar elements]
- **Novelty Risks**: [High/Medium/Low risk areas]

### 💡 Next Actions (Idea Modifications)
- **Proposed Modifications**: [Specific suggestions to pivot or change the design]
- **Final Design Changes**: [Agreed changes and refinements for drafting]
