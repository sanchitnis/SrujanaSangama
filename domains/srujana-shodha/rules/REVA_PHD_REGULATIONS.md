# PhD Scholar — REVA PhD Regulations Hard Rules

> Source: `references/reva-PhD-regulations.md` — REVA University Regulations Governing the Minimum Standards and Procedure for Award of Doctoral (Ph.D.) Degree, 2025.
> Every rule below includes the section number. Always cite the section when invoking a hard stop.

---

## Duration Rules

| Rule | Section | Enforcement |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum duration: **3 years** from provisional registration date (including coursework) | §4.1 | **Hard stop** — refuse to assist with thesis submission preparation if registration date is <3 years ago |
| Maximum duration: **6 years** normally; up to **8 years** in special cases (medical, pandemic) — requires committee approval (VC + Director R&D + Doctoral Committee) | §4.2 | Warning at year 5; hard stop advisory at year 6 without extension |
| Women candidates / persons with disability (>40%): **+2 years** relaxation on maximum | §4.4 | Apply automatically when candidate type is confirmed |
| Maternity / Child Care Leave: once during the programme, up to **240 days** | §4.4 | Advisory — inform when scholar mentions maternity/family circumstances |
| Maximum hard limit: **10 years** — registration annulled; scholar must re-apply | §4.5 | Hard stop — do not assist with any PhD work if 10-year limit is reached; direct to Director R&D |
| 4-year degree / other-domain / foreign candidate minimum: **4 years** | §4.6 | Hard stop — override the 3-year minimum for these candidate types |

## Credit Floor Rules (Coursework)

| Candidate Type | Credit Floor | Section |
|---|---|---|
| FT/PT standard (M.Tech/M.Sc or equivalent, normal pathway) | 18 credits | §9.7a |
| 4-year degree holder (B.Tech with ≥70% aggregate direct PhD) | 46 credits — 3 semesters | §9.7b |
| Industrial experience candidate (rich industry background, any Master's) | 30 credits | §9.7c |
| Foreign candidate / other-domain candidate | 50 credits | §9.7d |

**Hard stop:** Do not mark a scholar as having completed coursework if their confirmed credit count is below their pathway floor. Cite section number.

## Publication Minimums (Batch 2018+)

Scholar must satisfy **one** of the following options. At submission time, at least one publication must be **active**.

| Option | Requirement | Section |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | 3 peer-reviewed journals (Scopus / WoS / UGC) + 1 reputed conference | §14.1b |
| **B** | 2 peer-reviewed journals (Scopus / WoS / UGC) + 1 granted patent + 1 reputed conference | §14.1b |
| **C** | 2 Q1/Q2 journals + 1 reputed conference | §14.1b |

**Engineering & Applied Sciences floor:** ≥1 publication must be in a Q1/Q2/Q3 journal (§14.1b, final paragraph).

**Authorship:** Scholar = main author; guide/co-guide = co-authors. Additional co-authors require an NoC (§14.1, final paragraph).

**Pre-2018 batch:** ≥2 referred journal articles + 1 conference/seminar paper as main/co-author (§14.1a).

**Hard stop:** Do not assist with thesis submission preparation if publication minimum is not met. Cite §14.1.

## Pre-Submission Colloquium (§14.2)

**Mandatory — hard stop if bypassed.**

Process:
1. Scholar submits **letter of intent** to Director R&D Cell describing original contributions, enclosing publication acceptance letters or published papers + conference certificates
2. Director R&D requests BoS Chair to conduct an **internal review meeting**
3. If internal committee suggests corrections → scholar makes changes → appears again
4. Only after internal review clearance: **Pre-thesis Submission Colloquium** is organised by Doctoral Committee Chairperson
5. Colloquium is open to all faculty and scholars in the school
6. If Doctoral Committee is not satisfied → scholar must reappear after ≥1 month gap (§14.2f)

**Hard stop trigger:** If scholar asks to proceed to thesis submission without completing the pre-submission colloquium, stop and cite §14.2.

## Synopsis & Examiner Panel (§14.3)

After successful pre-thesis colloquium:
- Scholar submits 2 copies of Synopsis + soft copy + fees to Registrar (Evaluation) via guide, co-guide, and Director of School
- BoS Chair submits panel of **minimum 12 examiners** — 6 from Karnataka, 6 from outside Karnataka (≥1 from abroad where possible); minimum h-index of 5 recommended
- BoS Chair must complete this within **4 weeks** of communication from Director R&D (§14.3d)

## Thesis Submission Deadlines (§14.4)

| Batch | Academic Year | Last date to submit thesis (fee exemption) |
|---|---|---|
| 2014–2019 | June to May | 30 November |
| 2020 onwards | November to October | 30 April |

- Scholar has **6 months** from synopsis submission date to submit thesis (fee exemption window)
- If scholar enters a new academic year without submitting, tuition fee for the new year applies

## Plagiarism Ceiling (§14.4b-ii)

**Ceiling: 10%** similarity — **hard stop** if exceeded.

**Excluded from count:**
- Quoted text with permission and proper citation
- References, bibliography, ToC, preface, acknowledgements
- Scholar's own previously published work (with citation)
- Generic terms, laws, standards, standard symbols, standard equations

Scholar must provide undertaking + guide certificate of originality at thesis submission.

---

## Thesis Submission Package (§14.4)

- 5 bound copies of thesis + 1 electronic version (CD, PDF format)
- Certificate signed by scholar, guide, co-guide (if any), and Director of School
- NoC from any co-authors other than guide/co-guide
- Publication certificate from guide (§14.4b-i) confirming publications as per §14.1
- Undertaking from scholar confirming this thesis has not been submitted elsewhere (§14.4b-iii)

---

## Viva Voce (§15.4)

- **Open viva** — mandatory; held in presence of one external examiner + Doctoral Committee members + faculty + scholars of the school and other schools
- School may conduct one **mock viva** prior to the open viva (§15.4a)
- Viva may be held via **video conferencing** with additional fee (§15.4d)

---

## INFLIBNET Deposit (§16)

Electronic copy of the thesis must be submitted to INFLIBNET **before** the award of the PhD degree is announced.

---

## Change of Title / Guide (§18)

- Change of title: permitted **only before the pre-submission colloquium** (§18c) — hard stop if requested after
- Change of guide: generally not permissible; only in exceptional circumstances (death, disability, departure, interdisciplinary need, or legal actions) — route to Director R&D (§18d)
