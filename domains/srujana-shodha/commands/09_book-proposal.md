# Session Type: Book Proposal & Publication Strategy

**Purpose:** Help the scholar decide on the right book type, develop a book proposal, and plan the path to book publication.

**Estimated total time:** 45–60 minutes

---

## Phase 1 — Book Type Assessment (10 min)

Activate `book-agent.md` → book type assessment.

Ask:
1. *"What type of book output interests you — monograph, textbook, or contributing a chapter to someone else's volume?"*
2. *"Who is your primary reader — fellow researchers, students, practitioners?"*

Map responses to book types:

| Type | Best Fit |
|---|---|
| Research Monograph | Broad theoretical contribution; thesis-to-book conversion |
| Textbook | Strong teaching background; pedagogically rich content |
| Edited Volume | Strong research network; multi-perspective topic |
| Chapter Contribution | Early career; building visibility; fastest to publish |

Recommend the most suitable type for the scholar's current stage and contribution profile.

---

## Phase 2 — Thesis-to-Monograph Reframing (15 min)

If monograph is selected:

Review the key differences (thesis vs. monograph — from `book-agent.md`):
- Audience: doctoral committee → wider scholarly community
- Structure: objectives-driven → argument-driven
- Length: no limit → 80,000–120,000 words

Help the scholar reframe:
1. *"What is the single idea this book argues? (Not your objectives list — but a claim you are making about the world.)"*
2. Sketch the argument-driven chapter structure
3. Identify what to remove (thesis-specific appendices, committee forms, REVA certificates)
4. Identify what to expand (introduction, conclusion — most important sections)

---

## Phase 3 — Publisher Targeting (10 min)

Based on book type and research domain:

For CS research monographs:
- Top choices: Springer, ACM Books, MIT Press, Elsevier Computer Science
- India context: Springer India, PHI Learning, Wiley India

For textbooks (India academic market):
- PHI Learning, Pearson India, Cengage India, McGraw-Hill Education India, Wiley India

Generate a shortlist of 2–3 publishers. For each:
- Known series or imprints that match the topic
- Open access option availability
- Typical advance + royalty structure (if known)
- Submission process: manuscript sample + proposal, or proposal-first

---

## Phase 4 — Book Proposal Structure (15 min)

For the selected publisher, build the book proposal:

1. **Title and subtitle** — specific, searchable
2. **Abstract** (300 words — argument, significance, audience)
3. **Target readership and courses** — which UG/PG courses would adopt this?
4. **Chapter-by-chapter outline** (1 paragraph per chapter)
5. **Market analysis** — 3 competing books, why this one fills a different gap
6. **Sample chapter** — introduction or Chapter 1 (many publishers require this upfront)
7. **Author credentials** — publications, teaching, industry experience

Draft each section together or assign specific sections as homework.

---

## Phase 5 — Chapter Contribution Path (if selected instead) (10 min)

If the scholar wants to contribute a chapter to an edited volume:
1. Identify active calls for chapters (SSRN, ResearchGate, LinkedIn academic groups)
2. Help draft a chapter abstract (500–1000 words)
3. Plan a 6,000–10,000 word chapter if abstract is accepted
