# Session Type: Ikigai Audit — Research–Purpose Alignment Review

**Purpose:** Map the scholar's research to the four ikigai dimensions, produce an alignment score, identify tension areas, and generate renewal actions. Cadence: annual minimum, or triggered at biannual review or on-demand.

**Estimated total time:** 30–45 minutes

---

## Phase 1 — Trigger Context (2 min)

Ask: *"What prompted today's ikigai check — is this our regular biannual review, or did something make you want to reassess?"*

This helps calibrate tone: a routine audit is different from a crisis-triggered one.

If crisis-triggered: lead with wellbeing first — ask the scholar how they are doing before going into the framework.

---

## Phase 2 — Four-Quadrant Mapping (15 min)

Activate `ikigai-compass.md` → 4-quadrant exploration.

Work through each quadrant with care:

### Quadrant 1 — What You Love (Passion)
*"When you lose track of time doing computer science work, what are you usually doing? Not what you think you should enjoy — what actually absorbs you?"*

### Quadrant 2 — What You Are Good At (Strength)
*"What do people ask you for help with? What do you find easy that others seem to find hard?"*

### Quadrant 3 — What the World Needs (Mission)
*"If your research succeeds — 5 years from now — who benefits and how? Can you describe a person whose life is better because of your work?"*

### Quadrant 4 — What You Can Be Paid For (Vocation)
*"What kind of career do you see after the PhD? Industry, academia, government, startup? What role do you imagine yourself in?"*

---

## Phase 3 — Overlap Zone Analysis (10 min)

Synthesise the 4 quadrant responses:

```
## Ikigai Alignment Map — [Name] — [Date]

Passion: [scholar's words]
Strength: [scholar's words]
World-need: [scholar's words]
Vocation: [scholar's words]

Overlap zones:
- Passion ∩ Strength → [what emerges]
- Passion ∩ World-need → [what emerges]
- Strength ∩ Vocation → [what emerges]
- World-need ∩ Vocation → [what emerges]
- All four → [scholar's ikigai statement if visible, or "not yet clear"]
```

---

## Phase 4 — Research Alignment Assessment (5 min)

Assess how the current research topic maps to the ikigai centre:

**Green:** Research topic sits clearly at the intersection of all four quadrants — passion, strength, societal need, and career goal are coherent.

**Amber:** Partial alignment — identify the specific quadrant where the research falls short. Examples:
- Passion quadrant: scholar chose topic strategically but doesn't find it intrinsically interesting → suggest topic angle refinement
- Vocation quadrant: research has no clear career pathway scholar cares about → explore applied outcomes or career reframing
- World-need quadrant: research is technically sound but scholar can't articulate who it helps → help sharpen the impact story

**Red:** Weak alignment — research and the scholar's core values are misaligned. This is rare and serious. Surface gently: *"I'm noticing that your research topic and what matters most to you don't seem to be connecting strongly right now. Would it help to explore whether a refinement — not a restart — might change that?"*

---

## Phase 5 — Renewal Actions (5 min)

Based on alignment gaps, produce 1–3 renewal actions:

```
## Renewal Actions — [Date]
Alignment status: [Green / Amber — specific gap / Red — significant misalignment]

1. [specific action] — [rationale]
2. [specific action] — [rationale]
3. [specific action if needed] — [rationale]

Next ikigai audit: [next biannual review date]
```

Examples of renewal actions:
- Refine the research angle to include an application domain the scholar cares about
- Schedule one conversation with a person the research could benefit (grounds the world-need)
- Explore an industry collaboration or fieldwork component
- Revisit the topic shortlist from `topic-scout.md` if misalignment is deep

---

## Phase 6 — Disengagement Signal Check (3 min)

Per IKIGAI_ALIGNMENT.md: check for disengagement signals over the past 30–60 days:
- Repeated session cancellations
- Avoidance of specific research tasks
- Loss of curiosity
- Hostility toward research
- Change in communication tone

If 2+ signals present: flag (warning) and recommend an increased check-in cadence.
