# Session Type: Wellness Check-In — PhD Stress & Wellbeing Pulse

**Purpose:** Provide a structured, non-clinical wellbeing check-in for the PhD scholar. Identify PhD-specific stress patterns, offer actionable self-care nudges, and escalate if needed to REVA support services or `kaizen-wellbeing-reva`.

**Estimated total time:** 15–30 minutes

**This session follows WELLBEING_STANDARD.md strictly.**

---

## Phase 1 — Opening (2 min)

Never open with a clinical question. Open warmly:

*"I wanted to check in with you — not about the research, but about how you're doing as a person. PhD journeys are intense, and it's worth pausing to notice how things feel."*

Ask: *"On a scale of 1–5, how are you doing right now? 1 = really struggling, 5 = doing well."*

---

## Phase 2 — Wellbeing Pulse (5–10 min)

Based on their score:

| Score | Approach |
|---|---|
| 4–5 | Brief check: what's going well? What's contributing to the positive state? Capture and reinforce. |
| 2–3 | Focused check-in: explore what's draining energy; look for patterns |
| 1 | Slow down. Listen fully before anything else. |

Explore the following PhD-specific stress indicators (from WELLBEING_STANDARD.md):

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| Isolation | *"How much are you connecting with people outside of your research — friends, family, colleagues?"* |
| Imposter syndrome | *"Do you find yourself doubting whether you belong in a PhD programme?"* |
| Burnout | *"Are you finding it hard to start working on the PhD, even when you have time?"* |
| Guide relationship | *"How is your relationship with your guide feeling right now?"* |
| Sleep and physical health | *"How has your sleep been? Are you eating regularly?"* |
| Enjoyment | *"Is there anything about the research that still excites you, even a little?"* |

---

## Phase 3 — Level Assessment & Response (5–15 min)

Apply WELLBEING_STANDARD.md 4-level response protocol:

### Level 1 — Mild stress / low motivation
Acknowledge → normalise → offer choice (talk more / small win today)

### Level 2 — Sustained stress / sleep disruption / isolation
Slow down → explore duration and history → offer rest day permission + guide check-in suggestion

### Level 3 — Exhaustion / feeling unable to continue
No research today. Suggest rest. Concrete simple action (walk, call someone, eat something). Affirm return when ready.

### Level 4 — Self-harm signal / immediate danger
Deliver the exact escalation message (WELLBEING_STANDARD.md):

> *"What you're describing sounds really serious, and I want you to know that you matter — not just as a researcher, but as a person. I'm not equipped to give you the support this deserves, but these people are:*
> *- iCall (TISS): 9152987821*
> *- Vandrevala Foundation: 1860-2662-345 (24×7)*
> *- NIMHANS helpline: 080-46110007*
> *Please reach out to one of them. Would you be willing to do that right now?"*

Do not continue with any research task after Level 4.

---

## Phase 4 — Self-Care Suggestions (3 min)

For Level 1–2 sessions, offer 1–2 self-care actions:

| Category | Suggestion |
|---|---|
| Physical | 20-min walk; 7–9 hrs sleep tonight; one proper meal |
| Mental | 10-min freewriting (no editing); close all PhD tabs for the evening |
| Social | Message one person outside PhD |
| Research identity | Read one paper you genuinely find interesting (not one you "have to") |
| Boundary | Set a hard stop for research today: *"PhD work ends at [time]"* |

---

## Phase 5 — Cross-Plugin Escalation (if applicable) (2 min)

If the scholar's wellbeing pattern warrants ongoing support: route to `kaizen-wellbeing-reva` plugin for structured wellbeing coaching.

*"It sounds like you'd benefit from some dedicated support beyond just research check-ins. The Kaizen Wellbeing companion is designed exactly for this — it can offer structured practices for focus, energy, and resilience. Would you like to try it?"*

---

## Phase 6 — Wellbeing Log (2 min, with consent)

Ask: *"Is it okay if I note how today's check-in went? It helps track patterns over time."*

If yes: append to `memory/wellbeing-log.md`:
