COURSE OVERVIEW: Joyful Learning (2 Credits)

For B.Tech CSE First-Year Students

In an AI-powered, fast-changing world, the ability to learn how to learn is a vital lifelong skill. This course empowers students to build that skill joyfully—through exploration, reflection, and self-directed growth—rather than through pressure or external rewards.

Unlike traditional foundation or study-skills courses, Joyful Learning reimagines education as a space for play, curiosity, and self-expression. Today’s students are often overwhelmed by academic expectations and packed schedules, leaving little room to discover how they want to learn and who they want to become. This course creates that space—where learning feels like play, and growth is guided by purpose rather than pressure.

To help students reflect on and measure their personal development, a set of psychometric assessments will be administered at the beginning and end of the course. These are for self-tracking only and are not used for grading:

Core Assessments:

Grit Scale (Angela Duckworth) – Measures perseverance and sustained passion.

Self-Regulation Questionnaire (SRQ) – Assesses emotional and behavioral self-management.

Growth Mindset Scale (Carol Dweck) – Evaluates belief in the ability to grow through effort.

Big Five Personality Traits – Conscientiousness & Openness subscales – Reflects learning behavior and curiosity.

Learning Autonomy Index – Measures students' ownership of their learning journey.

Meaningful Participation \- Measures the enthusiasm and passion to work both as individual and as part of a team

Optional Assessments:

PERMA Profiler (Positive Psychology) – Evaluates well-being across Positive Emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, and Accomplishment.

Creative Self-Efficacy Scale – Assesses confidence in one’s own ability to think creatively and solve problems.

These assessments serve as a mirror for students to understand themselves better, set personal goals, and take ownership of their learning process.

The course draws inspiration from behavioral science and habit-formation frameworks like Atomic Habits. Students set individual or team goals, participate in reflective and engaging activities, and receive mentoring support in a fun, yet disciplined environment. Facilitated by Computer Science faculty for over 1,000 students, the course does not require expertise in psychology—instead, it nurtures a peer-driven culture of curiosity, self-awareness, and joyful exploration.

By the end of the course, students will not only gain lifelong learning habits but also experience learning as something they choose—with joy, freedom, and purpose.

COURSE OBJECTIVE(S):

1. Engage students to understand and appreciate the technology behind everyday things/passions with effective storytelling  
2. Engage students in problem solving at an individual and team level (through games)  
3. Enable students to explore techniques/methods for time management, learning and impactful research using the latest AI techniques  
4. Familiarize students with sensitive topics like ethics, professionalism, resilience, etc and make available a toolkit for them to navigate  
5. Inculcate good habit forming behavior that can be pursued throughout their studies and professional career  to help cope and excel
