> **[COURSE OVERVIEW]{.underline}**
>
> This course introduces fundamental concepts of Vector Calculus,
> Differential Calculus, Integral Calculus and Linear Algebra, which
> form the foundation for higher studies in mathematics, computer
> science, and Design engineering. The course covers essential vector
> operations such as velocity, acceleration, gradient, divergence. In
> Differential Calculus, students learn higher-order derivatives,
> Intermediate Value Theorem, Differentiability, Rolle's Theorem, Mean
> Value Theorem, and Taylor's formula, Functions of several variables,
> Limits and Continuity, Geometric representation of partial and total
> derivatives, Derivatives of composite functions. In Integral Calculus,
> students learn about Multiple Integrals and its applications. Linear
> Algebra topics include rank of matrices, echelon forms, system of
> linear equations, Gauss elimination, LU decomposition, linear
> transformations, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, diagonalization, and
> the Rayleigh power method.
>
> The knowledge gained from this course is vital in computer science
> applications such as machine learning, optimization, data mining,
> computer graphics, scientific computing, cryptography, image
> processing, and network algorithms, Robotics and Automation, agri-tech
> and defence technology. By the end of the course, students will have a
> strong foundation in calculus and linear algebra for solving
> real-world computational and mathematical problems.
>
> **[Pre-requisites:]{.underline}**
>
> **Differential Calculus:** Requires a mastery of Algebra (factoring
> and exponents), Trigonometry (identities and the unit circle), and the
> concept of Limits.
>
> **Integral Calculus:** Depends heavily on Differential Calculus
> (reverse power/chain rules), Partial Fractions, and familiarity with
> Logarithmic/Exponential functions.
>
> **Vector Calculus:** Built upon Basic Vector Algebra (dot and cross
> products), 3D Coordinate Systems (Cartesian, Cylindrical, Spherical),
> and Partial Differentiation.
>
> **Linear Algebra:** Needs a foundation in Systems of Equations
> (elimination/substitution), Matrix Arithmetic, and the geometric
> understanding of Lines and Planes.
>
> **[Topics:]{.underline}**

**[Unit -- I]{.underline}**

**Differential Calculus:** Limits and Continuity of functions defined on
intervals, Intermediate Value Theorem, Differentiability, Rolle's
Theorem, Mean Value Theorem, and Taylor's formula, Functions of several
variables, Limits and Continuity, Geometric representation of partial
and total derivatives, Derivatives of composite functions.

**[Unit -- II]{.underline}**

> **Vector Calculus:** Directional Derivatives, Gradient, Lagrange
> Multipliers, Optimization Problems
>
> **Integral Calculus:** Evaluation of double integrals and triple
> integrals, Evaluation of double integrals over the given region, by
> changing the order of integration, By change of variables, Evaluation
> of Line and Surface Integrals. Application to area and volume --
> illustrative examples.

**[Unit -- III]{.underline}**

> **Linear Algebra-I:** Echelon form, Normal form of a matrix, Rank of
> Matrix, Consistency of the system of homogeneous and non-homogeneous
> linear equations, Gauss elimination to solve system of equations, and
> LU decomposition method.
>
> Linear transformation, Eigen values and Eigen Vectors up to 3\*3
> matrices, Diagonalization for 2\*2 matrices, Rayleigh power method to
> determiner largest Eigen value and the corresponding Eigen vector.

**[Unit -- IV]{.underline}**

**Linear Algebra-II:** Vector spaces, General properties of vector
spaces, Vector subspaces, Algebra of subspaces, and Linear combination
of vectors. Linear span, linear sum of two subspaces, Linear
independence and dependence of vectors, Basis of vector space. Finite
dimensional vector spaces, Dimension of a vector space, Dimension of a
subspace. Linear transformations, Linear operators, Range and null space
of linear transformation, Rank and nullity of linear transformations.

> **COURSE OBJECTIVE (S):**
>
> **Students are able to learn**

1.  The fundamental concepts of differential calculus, limits and
    continuity.

2.  The fundamental concepts of vector calculus and multiple integrals.

3.  The concepts of eigenvalue/eigenvector for various engineering
    problems.

4.  The techniques like diagonalization and vector space.
